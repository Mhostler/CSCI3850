<TITLE> German leftists battle police on Labor Day</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> German leftists battle police on Labor Day</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-GERMANY-RIOT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BERLIN (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  About 2,000 radical leftists fought May Day street battles with police in Berlin late Sunday and early Monday, authorities said. </P>
<P> A police spokesman said 72 officers were injured before the rioters were dispersed with water cannon and tear gas and 36 people were detained on charges of disturbing the peace, damaging property and resisting arrest. </P>
<P> Six officers were still in hospital Monday. </P>
<P> German leftists traditionally clash with police on May 1, celebrated as Labor Day. </P>
<P> About 150 young radicals gathered Sunday night in the Prenzlauer Berg section and hurled stones and bottles at police. </P>
<P> The crowd that grew to about 2,000 fought pitched battles with police and erected barricades. </P>
