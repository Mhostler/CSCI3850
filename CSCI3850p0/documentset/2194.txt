<TITLE> Kabul rocketing kills 35</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Kabul rocketing kills 35</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AFGHAN-FIGHTING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KABUL, Afghanistan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Thirty-five people were killed and 98 wounded in rocket attacks on the capital Monday, hospital sources said. </P>
<P> Officials of President Burhanuddin Rabbani's administration blamed the rocket attacks on factions opposing the president. </P>
<P> Twenty people were killed in a bus and taxi when two rockets hit Kabul's Kolala Pushta street, witnesses said. </P>
<P> The rockets came from south and southwest of Kabul and hit all over the war-shattered city, they said. Hospital sources said a total of 35 people died in the attacks. </P>
<P> Presidential jets bombed opposition positions in southwestern Kabul but there was no immediate information about casualties from there, official sources said. </P>
<P> Forty-five people were killed and 150 wounded in rocket attacks, shelling and bombing Friday. </P>
<P> Another 15 people were reported to have been killed and 43 wounded in opposition rocket attacks Sunday. </P>
<P> Factional fighting has killed more than 12,000 people in Kabul since Islamic guerrillas took power from a collapsed communist government in April 1992 after 14 years of civil war. </P>
