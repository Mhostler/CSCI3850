<TITLE> Deng's daughter says Chinese leader in good health</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Deng's daughter says Chinese leader in good health</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-DENG </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TOKYO, April 6 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  One of the daughters of China's paramount leader Deng Xiaoping said on Thursday her 90-year-old father was in good health and was living at the family home. </P>
<P> ``My father is quite well,'' the Chinese leader's second daughter, Deng Nan, was quoted as saying in an interview with Japan's Kyodo news agency. She told Kyodo that Deng Xiaoping was living at the Deng family residence in Beijing. </P>
<P> The comments are the latest in a series of reassuring statements by Chinese officials about Deng Xiaoping, who was recently rumoured to have suffered a decline in health. </P>
<P> Deng Nan was speaking during a visit to Japan in her capacity as vice-minister of China's State Science and Technology Commission. </P>
<P> Deng Nan is visiting Japan from April 2 to April 11 to observe Japanese environmental policies and facilities. </P>
<P> On Tuesday, Vice Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan told Japanese reporters in Hangzhou that Deng Xiaoping was in ``good health for an old man of 90.'' </P>
