<TITLE> Light earthquake rattles northern California</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Light earthquake rattles northern California</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CALIFORNIA-QUAKE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SAN FRANCISCO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A 4.1-magnitude earthquake struck northern California Tuesday and was followed by a series of light aftershocks, but seismologists said there were no reports of damages or injuries. </P>
<P> The quake was felt by residents of the Clear Lake, Calif. area about 50 miles north of San Francisco. </P>
<P> The quake was centered 17 miles southeast of Lakeport. </P>
<P> It struck at 3:09 p.m. (6:09 p.m. EST) and was followed by a series of light aftershocks. </P>
<P> U.S. Geological Survey scientists in Menlo Park, Calif., said there were no reports of damages or injury. </P>
