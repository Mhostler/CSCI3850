<TITLE> Cowboys named most valuable franchise for third straight year</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Cowboys named most valuable franchise for third straight year</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-TEAMS-VALUE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The National Football League Dallas Cowboys is the most valuable franchise in North American professional sports for the third consecutive year, according to an annual survey by Financial World Magazine. </P>
<P> The Cowboys franchise is valued at $238 million, up 25 percent from the $190 million evaluation last year, according to the survey appearing in the May 9 issue. </P>
<P> Two other NFL teams, the Miami Dolphins and the San Francisco 49ers, tied for second at $186 million. </P>
<P> The New York Yankees were listed as the top baseball team and fourth most valuable franchise overall at $185 million. </P>
<P> The NFL Philadelphia Eagles ranked fifth at $182 million and the top National Basketball Association team, the Detroit Pistons, ranked sixth at $180 million. </P>
<P> The NBA New York Knicks were seventh at $173 million, with three more NFL clubs -- the Buffalo Bills, Kansas City Chiefs and New Orleans Saints -- rounding out the top 10. </P>
<P> The NBA Los Angeles Lakers were in 11th place at $169 million, followed by the NFL New York Giants at $168 million, which represented a five percent drop from last year. </P>
<P> The top National Hockey League club was the Detroit Red Wings, which landed 48th, valued at $124 million. </P>
<P> According to Financial World, the Cowboys' revenue from the rental of 280 luxury suites allowed them to generate $37 million in revenue, more than double the figure of any other football team. </P>
<P> The Baltimore Orioles also climbed dramatically to 14th overall at $164 million due to the rental of 122 luxury suites at Camden Yards. </P>
<P> The least valuable franchise among the four major North American sports was the Winnipeg Jets at $35 million. </P>
<P> Among the most significant drops in value, baseball's Milwaukee Brewers were down 22 percent to $75 million, and the San Diego Padres were down 13 percent to $74 million. </P>
