<TITLE> Israel's Peres meets Tunisian and Qatati ministers</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Israel's Peres meets Tunisian and Qatati ministers</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-UN-MIDEAST-PERES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>UNITED NATIONS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Israeli Foreign Minister Shimon Peres held meetings Saturday with the foreign ministers of Tunisia and Qatar, an Israeli source said. </P>
<P> Israel has no diplomatic relations with either Arab country. All three ministers are in New York for the U.N. General Asssembly. </P>
<P> It was Peres's first meeeting with Tunisian Foreign Minister Habib Ben Yahia, with whom he was reported to have discussed the possibility of establishing low level diplomatic relations. No immediate decision was taken. </P>
<P> Israel is known to be hoping to arrange an exchange of liaison offices with Tunisia, similar to those which Israel and Morocco recently decided to establish. </P>
<P> Qatari Foreign Minister Sheik Hamad al-Thani was reported to have told Peres Qatar would take part in a high level conference to be held in Casablanca, Morocco, at the end of October to discuss Middle East economic development. </P>
<P> Peres updated al-Thani on the latest developments in the Middle East peace process. </P>
<P> The Israeli foreign minister also welcomed a decision announced Friday by the six-nation Gulf Cooperation Council, including Qatar, to ease the four-decade old Arab boycott of Israel by dropping a ban on dealings with companies that do business with the Jewish state. </P>
