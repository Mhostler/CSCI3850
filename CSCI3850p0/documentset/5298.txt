<TITLE> Yemenis in Aden protest gasoline price hikes</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Yemenis in Aden protest gasoline price hikes</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-YEMEN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ADEN (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Police used tear gas and fired shots in the air to disperse protestors in Aden Wednesday after the government doubled gasoline prices as part of a reform program for its ailing economy. </P>
<P> Witnesses said demonstrators blocked roads and burned tyres in riots which erupted in the Sheikh Othman suburb and the affluent Khor Maksar area after the government doubled prices of benzene, cooking gas and other energy products Tuesday night. </P>
<P> They said four people were wounded. </P>
<P> Police in Sheikh Othman used tear gas to disperse the demonstrators and fired shots in the air to disperse hundreds of students in he affluent suburb of Khor Maksar demonstrating against a doubling of public transport fares. </P>
<P> But the city quietened down after the government announced austerity measures affecting some more privileged civil servants. </P>
<P> Official Sanaa Radio said a few hours after the riots broke out the cabinet had decided to cut the number of Yemeni diplomats abroad to no more than five at each embassy and cancel all military, press, cultural and commercial attache jobs. </P>
<P> It also asked the finance minister to ``to cut expenses of extra wages, rewards, compensations and conferences,'' saying official delegations should not have more than three members and should not spend more than 10 days abroad. </P>
<P> Yemen, for years suffering a deep economic crisis, annual inflation of 100 percent and high unemployment, announced on Tuesday it was raising subsidised domestic energy prices as part of its economic reforms. </P>
