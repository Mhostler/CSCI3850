<TITLE> S.Korea says no to joint war games with U.S.</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> S.Korea says no to joint war games with U.S.</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-GAMES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SEOUL, South Korea (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Prime Minister Lee Hong-koo said Wednesday South Korea was not planning to hold a major military exercise with the United States, a move which will help to improve strained relations with the communist North. </P>
<P> Lee told foreign correspondents that Seoul was trying to maintain a low-key policy over contentious issues such as the annual Team Spirit U.S.-South Korean war games, in a bid to persuade Pyongyang to ease its international isolation. </P>
