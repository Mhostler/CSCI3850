<TITLE> Koran reciters win gold coins at Iran competition</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Koran reciters win gold coins at Iran competition</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-IRAN-KORAN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NICOSIA, Cyprus (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A Portuguese and a Thai were among Koran reciters, memorizers and interpreters who won gold coins in a five-day competition that ended in Tehran Wednesday, Iranian television said. </P>
<P> It showed winners receiving their prizes from Iranian officials and shaking hands with Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who spoke on the importance of promoting the study of the Muslim holy book. </P>
<P> Top prizes were 35 Azadi gold coins worth $100 each. The leading winners, from among dozens of Iranian and 69 foreign competitors, were from Portugal, Thailand, Bangladesh, Yemen, Syria, Morocco and Iran, the television said. </P>
<P> All the winners shown on television were men. The report did not say if women also competed. </P>
