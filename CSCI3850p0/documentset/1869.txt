<TITLE> Saudi Arabia concerned at Iraq troop report-agency</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Saudi Arabia concerned at Iraq troop report-agency</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-IRAQ-KUWAIT-SAUDI 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>(Eds; Adds contacts between Kuwait and Riyadh)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>KUWAIT (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Saudi Arabia is concerned about reports of Iraqi troop movements toward Kuwait and supports the security of the emirate against any external threat, the official Kuwait News Agency reported Saturday. </P>
<P> The agency, quoting a dispatch by the official Saudi Press Agency (SPA), said: ``Saudi Arabia said on Friday night that it supports Kuwait's security and stability against any external threats.'' </P>
<P> King Fahd ``was concerned over news reports of Iraqi troop movements towards the south near the Kuwaiti border,'' it quoted SPA as saying. </P>
<P> U.S. officials said Friday that Iraq was moving troops towards the emirate. </P>
<P> ``Saudi Arabia stresses the need for Iraq to implement the U.N. Security Council resolutions which it signed,'' SPA quoted a Saudi official source as saying, the Kuwait agency said. </P>
<P> Kuwaiti Foreign Minister and Deputy Prime Minister Sheikh Sabah al-Ahmad al-Sabah discussed ``Iraqi threats against Kuwait'' in a telephone conversation with Saudi Defense Minister Prince Sultan bin Abdulaziz, the Kuwaiti agency reported. </P>
<P> The Saudi minister expressed Riyadh's full support for Kuwait, it said. The two ministers also discussed the possibility of holding a special meeting of Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries to discuss Iraqi actions. </P>
<P> The GCC comprises Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, the United Arab Emirates, Oman, Qatar and Bahrain. </P>
<P> Sheikh Sabah also had telephone discussions with Egyptian Foreign Minister Amr Moussa, who ``expressed unlimited backing for Kuwait,'' and Arab League secretary-general Esmat Abdel Maguid, the agency reported.  And the official Kuwait News Agency (KUNA) reported Saturday from Cairo that the Arab League is profoundly concerned over the safety and territorial integrity of Kuwait following reports of the Iraqi troop buildup. </P>
<P> League Secretary-General Esmat Abdel-Maguid said in a statement he was making extensive contacts through the region to try to contain the situation, KUNA said from Cairo where the league is based. </P>
