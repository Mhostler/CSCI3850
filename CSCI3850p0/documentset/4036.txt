<TITLE> Knicks take their turn as inhospitable hosts</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Knicks take their turn as inhospitable hosts</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NBA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> Ewing scored 35 points and grabbed 15 rebounds as the Knicks avenged a 106-85 loss at Phoenix last month. </P>
<P> ``It was a total team effort,'' said Ewing. ``We knew we had to come out and play well in our building. It's still a long way to go, so we have to keep getting all the wins we can.'' </P>
<P> The win was the 15th in 17 games for the surging Knicks (27-14), who had a six-game winning streak snapped Friday by Charlotte. </P>
<P> For Phoenix (33-9), the loss snapped a seven-game winning streak and nine-game road winning streak. </P>
<P> ``We weren't very good today and you get beat when you aren't very good,'' said Suns' coach Paul Westphal. </P>
<P> Ewing hit 13-of-22 shots from the field and had 12 of his points in the third quarter when the Knicks outscored Phoenix, 29-16, to build their largest lead at 79-58. </P>
<P> John Starks scored 22 points and Derek Harper added 15 for the Knicks. The Knicks outrebounded Phoenix by a whopping 62-36 margin. Anthony Mason led the Knicks with 19 rebounds. </P>
<P> Danny Ainge led Phoenix with 22 points. Charles Barkley had 20 points for the Suns, but was held to just four points in the second half. </P>
<P> In Chicago, the Bulls raced to a 30-9 lead in the first nine minutes of the game and coasted to a 116-94 rout of the Golden State Warriors. </P>
<P> Scottie Pippen returned after a one-game suspension for tossing a chair after being ejected from a game and nearly recorded a triple double with 27 points, 11 rebounds and eight assists despite sitting out the entire fourth quarter. </P>
<P> B.J. Armstrong scored 16 of his 19 points in the first quarter when the Bulls built a 35-13 lead. </P>
<P> Tim Hardaway led Golden State with 18 points. The loss was the 12th in 14 games for the fading Warriors. </P>
