<TITLE> SOLARZ NAMED TO RUN U.S. FUND FOR CENTRAL ASIA</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> SOLARZ NAMED TO RUN U.S. FUND FOR CENTRAL ASIA</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CIS-FUND </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  President Clinton Friday named former New York Congressman Stephen Solarz chairman of a new Central Asian-American Enterprise Fund to promote investments in the former Soviet republics of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan and Turkmenistan. </P>
<P> The White House said in a statment that the United States plans to capitalize the fund with $150 million in foreign assistance appropriations over the next three to four years. </P>
<P> It will have the authority to to make investments and loans as well as offer technical assistance to promote prive companies and entrepreneurs. </P>
