<TITLE> Bahrainis admit torching electrical cables</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Bahrainis admit torching electrical cables</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BAHRAIN-UNREST </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MANAMA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Bahrain said Tuesday that three Bahrainis charged with sabotage have admitted setting fire to electrical cables last March in anti-government protests. </P>
<P> The official Gulf news agency quoted an Interior Ministry source as saying Ali Mohammad Ahmed al-Mahdi, Ali Ibrahim Ali and Samir Ahmed Ali Hussain admitted before a judge that they torched the cables running across Sitra causeway on March 16. </P>
<P> The three were the last to be charged of dozens accused of sabotage in the protests which began on December 5 demanding the release of political detainees and the restoration of parliament, dissolved in 1975. </P>
<P> Monday, 10 Bahrainis charged with sabotage were sentenced to three to seven years in jail and fined a total of $320,000, in the second such reported case in months of protests in Bahrain. </P>
<P> Last week, the state security court sentenced another Bahraini charged with sabotage to 10 years in jail and $143,000 fines. </P>
<P> Hundreds of people have been detained and dozens of them are expected to stand trial for taking part in the protests. At least 10 civilians and three policemen were killed in the unrest. </P>
