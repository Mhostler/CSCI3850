<TITLE> Japan invites South Africa's Mandela for visit</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Japan invites South Africa's Mandela for visit</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SAFRICA-JAPAN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>JOHANNESBURG, South Africa (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Japan has invited South Africa's President Nelson Mandela to pay a state visit to Tokyo in early July, a top Japanese government official said. </P>
<P> Hakuo Yanagisawa, parliamentary vice-minister for foreign affairs, said he had handed a letter from Prime Minister Tomiichi Murayama to Mandela during South African Freedom Day celebrations Thursday. </P>
<P> Yanagisawa told a news briefing Thursday night the date for Mandela's visit had still to be set, but the invitation was for early July. The Japanese official left for Zimbabwe on Friday. </P>
<P> Mandela visited Japan as head of the African National Congress before his inauguration as South Africa's first black president in May last year. </P>
