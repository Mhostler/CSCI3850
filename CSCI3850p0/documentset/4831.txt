<TITLE> U.S. magician plans North Korea performance</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.S. magician plans North Korea performance</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-MAGIC </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SEOUL (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  David Copperfield, the world's richest magician, is planning a show in communist North Korea in July on the first anniversary of the death of ``Great Leader'' Kim Il-sung, a leading South Korean daily said Friday. </P>
<P> The Chosun Ilbo said the North was negotiating with an unnamed U.S. sponsor on ways to transport about 60 tons of equipment to the show in Pyongyang. </P>
<P> The daily, quoting an unnamed official dealing with the event in South Korea, said the North hoped the Seoul government would allow the equipment to be transported via the inter-Korean border village of Panmunjom. </P>
<P> South Korean officials believe Kim Jong-il, Kim Il-sung's son and political heir, might formally succeed his father as national leader shortly after the first anniversary his death. </P>
<P> Copperfield, estimated by Forbes Magazine to be one of the 10 richest entertainers in the world with an annual income of $20 million, does up to 500 live shows a year. </P>
<P> The magician was unavailable for comment. </P>
