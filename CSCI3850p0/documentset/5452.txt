<TITLE> Bonn, Geneva in run-off to host U.N. climate body</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Bonn, Geneva in run-off to host U.N. climate body</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CLIMATE-SEAT 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>(Adds Toronto dropping out)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>BERLIN (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Uruguayan and Canadian cities of Montevideo and Toronto dropped out of a race to host the secretariat of the U.N. climate convention Tuesday leaving two cities -- Bonn and Geneva -- still in the running. </P>
<P> Montevideo dropped out after coming last in a first round of informal voting at the U.N. climate conference on where the permanent headquarters should be based, and Toronto came last in the second round. The interim secretariat is now in Geneva. </P>
<P> ``There is an understanding among all the cities to support the consensus procedure,'' committee chairman Raul Estrada Oyuela said in response to an enquiry. </P>
<P> He said the run-off between Geneva and Bonn would take place Wednesday from 3 p.m., (9 a.m. EST) to 4:30 p.m. (12:30 p.m. </P>
<P> The informal, confidential voting process was intended to eliminate candidate cities one-by-one, leaving one city with an absolute majority of votes. This city will be offered to the plenary meeting for approval by consensus. </P>
<P> The conference, which aims to extend pledges made under the framework convention signed at the Rio Earth Summit in 1992, decided Tuesday that the new secretariat will have a staff of between 43 and 49. </P>
<P> It will have a budget for 1996 and 1997 of about $19 million. </P>
