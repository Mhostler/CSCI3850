<TITLE> SENATE MAY WORK 24-HOURS A DAY ON HEALTH CARE</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> SENATE MAY WORK 24-HOURS A DAY ON HEALTH CARE</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-HEALTH-CONGRESS-DELAYS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Senate Majority Leader George Mitchell said Monday he would keep the Senate in session day and night if Republicans continued to balk at casting votes on the health care reform bill. </P>
<P> The Senate has spent a week giving opening statements on the complex health bill. Republicans say they must analyze it before voting, but Democrats accuse them of stalling. </P>
