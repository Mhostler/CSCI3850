<TITLE> MINER DIES IN KENTUCKY'S 7TH MINING FATALITY IN '94</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> MINER DIES IN KENTUCKY'S 7TH MINING FATALITY IN '94</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-MINER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BARBOURVILLE, Ky (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A cave-in killed a miner in southeastern Kentucky Tuesday. It was the state's seventh coal mine fatality this year. </P>
<P> Keith Gibson, 22, of Knox County, was trapped in Minton Hickory No. 5 Mine in Knox County, Kentucky since Monday night when the roof he was bolting collapsed. </P>
<P> The miner was trapped about 7,200 feet into the mine under a large boulder. </P>
<P> Officials said rescue efforts to free the man were hampered by smaller active cave-ins. </P>
<P> Gibson was prounounced dead Tuesday afternoon when mining officials were finally able to reach him. </P>
