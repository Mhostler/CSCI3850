<TITLE> Students injured in possible mace attack</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Students injured in possible mace attack</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MACE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Dozens of students and teachers were injured Friday when what was believed to be mace was sprayed inside a junior high school, officials said. </P>
<P> All 1,600 students in the school in the New York City borough of Queens had to be evacuated, they said. </P>
<P> An Emergency Medical Services spokesman said 73 people were injured, including 54 children, suffering from breathing difficulties and irritation to the nose and lungs. Of those injured, 65 people were taken to local hospitals, he said. </P>
<P> A chemical believed to be mace or pepper mace was sprayed inside the school building at about 10 a.m., Fire Department officials said. Police said there were no arrests in the case. </P>
<P> The Fire Department's Hazardous Materials unit was investigating what was the second such attack in New York in less than 24 hours. Four teen-agers were arrested Thursday after allegedly spraying mace on board a crowded evening rush-hour subway train. Nine passengers, including two children, were injured in the subway incident and treated at nearby hospitals. </P>
