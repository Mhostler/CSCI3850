<TITLE> Pioneer woman combat pilot killed in crash</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pioneer woman combat pilot killed in crash</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PILOT-CRASH </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Lt. Kara Hultgreen, the first woman to qualify as a Navy fighter pilot, was lost at sea after her F-14A Tomcat crashed off the coast of Southern California, the Defense Department said Wednesday. </P>
<P> Her plane, based at Miramar Naval Air Station, crashed Tuesday afternoon. Three ships and helicopters searched the area but could not find Hultgreen, 29, a native of San Antonio, Texas. </P>
<P> Radar intercept officer Lt. Matthew Klemish was rescued immediately after the crash and is reported in excellent condition, the Navy said. </P>
