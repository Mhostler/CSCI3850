<TITLE> Israel, Jordan meet on Rift Valley project</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Israel, Jordan meet on Rift Valley project</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MIDEAST-JORDAN-ISRAEL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>AMMAN, April 2 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Jordanian and Israeli officials met on Sunday to chart joint strategy for multi-billion-dollar plans to develop the Rift Valley separating the two countries, delegates said. </P>
<P> They were meeting ahead of a meeting with U.S. officials in Amman on Monday. Washington, a key promoter of the plan, has promised to help raise funds to provide benefits from Jordan's October 26 peace treaty with Israel. </P>
<P> Delegates said the meeting of the Jordan Rift Valley's Steering Committee at Jordan's Red Sea port of Aqaba would also prepare for a regional economic conference in Jordan, expected to be held in late October. </P>
<P> It follows a similar meeting in Casablanca in November that drew world leaders and businessmen to discuss investment in the Middle East following moves toward peace. </P>
<P> Jordan and Israel are seeking massive funds to develop the Rift Valley. The plan includes a $3 billion canal to bring Red Sea water to the Dead Sea. </P>
