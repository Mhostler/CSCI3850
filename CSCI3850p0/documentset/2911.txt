<SUBJECT> BC-LAURO-ITALY-FIRE -2 ROME </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> The Naples-based Star Lauro company, owner of the vessel, said the 600 passengers and all crew were safe. The number of crew was not clear, but Italian radio put the total at 350. </P>
<P> The ship sent out a satellite distress signal after a blaze broke out on board off the coast of East Africa. </P>
<P> ``They have told us that the fire is uncontrollable. The ship is listing 20 degrees to the left,'' an Italian port authority spokesman told Italian radio. He put the vessel's position at 250 miles south of the Horn of Africa and said all passengers had taken to lifeboats. </P>
<P> The radio said only one officer had stayed on board the ship. It left the north Italian port of Genoa on Nov. 19 and had been heading for Haifa in Israel. </P>
