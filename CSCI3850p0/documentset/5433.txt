<TITLE> Police detain 84 in troubled Egyptian town</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Police detain 84 in troubled Egyptian town</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-ARRESTS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAIRO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Egyptian police have detained 84 suspected Muslim militants in the southern town of Samalout for questioning since three policeman and a woman were killed in attacks there last week, security sources said Tuesday. </P>
<P> Samalout, in the Nile Valley 120 miles south of Cairo, remains under the night curfew which the police imposed after the three separate shootings, they added. </P>
<P> Suspected militants killed two policemen on guard duty outside a church and one at Samalout railway station Friday. The woman was shot by accident Thursday and died of her wounds Saturday. </P>
<P> Samalout is in Minya province, the center of the conflict between the police and the Gama'a al-Islamiya (Islamic Group), the main militant group fighting to overthrow the government and set up a strict Islamic state in Egypt. </P>
<P> More than 700 people have been killed in political violence in the country since the conflict began three years ago. </P>
