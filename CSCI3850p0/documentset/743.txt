<TITLE> WHEREABOUTS UNKNOWN OF MAN IN BROOKLYN STABBING CASE</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> WHEREABOUTS UNKNOWN OF MAN IN BROOKLYN STABBING CASE</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-MURDER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ATLANTA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The whereabouts of the one teen-ager charged in charged in connection with the fatal 1991 stabbing of a Hasidic scholar in New York during four days of riots, were unknown Friday. </P>
<P> Lemrick Nelson Jr, 18, is being prosecuted as a minor on federal charges he violated the man's civil rights and therefore Thursday's hearing before a U.S. magistrate was closed to the public and all papers were sealed. </P>
<P> Nelson was acquitted on state murder charges in 1992 of the fatal stabbing of Yankel Rosenbaum, a 29-year-old Hasidic scholar visiting from Australia, during four days of riots in Brooklyn's Crown Heights section in 1991. </P>
<P> Nelson moved to Atlanta after the acquittal. His lawyer, Tony Axam said Friday he did not know where his client was. </P>
<P> Nelson emerged from hearing, but did not speak to reporters. </P>
<P> Rosenbaum was stabbed during a flare-up of violence described as the worst episode of racial unrest in New York in two decades. The disturbances were triggered by the death of a seven-year-old black boy, Gavin Cato, after a Hasidic driver lost control of his car and hit the boy. A second child was also injured in that accident. </P>
