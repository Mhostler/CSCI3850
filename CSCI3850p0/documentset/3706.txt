<TITLE> Chronology of attacks against Popes</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Chronology of attacks against Popes</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-POPE-ATTACKS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>VATICAN CITY (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Philippines President Fidel Ramos said Wednesday police had arrested two people suspected of posing a threat to Pope John Paul's security during a visit to Manila which starts Thursday. </P>
<P></P>
<P> NOVEMBER 27, 1970 - A Bolivian painter, Benjamin Mendoza, tries to stab Pope Paul VI at Manila airport. The pope is grazed in the chest by the knife. </P>
<P> FEBRUARY 16, 1981 - A grenade goes off in a stadium in Karachi, Pakistan, before the arrival of Pope John Paul to say mass during a stopover on his way to Japan. </P>
<P> The man carrying the grenade in his pocket is killed. The blast occurs far from the spot where the Pontiff was to say mass, leaving it unclear whether the bomb was intended for him. </P>
<P> MAY 13, 1981 - Turkish gunman Mehmet Ali Agca shoots and seriously injures Pope John Paul at his weekly general audience. The pope is saved by emergency surgery and Agca is later sentenced to life imprisonment. </P>
<P> MAY 12, 1982 - Dissident traditionalist Spanish priest Juan Fernandez Khron tries to stab Pope John Paul with a bayonet as the pontiff is saying thanks at the Fatima shrine in Portugal for surviving the attempt on his life in Rome the year before. </P>
<P> MARCH 29, 1986 - Three Bulgarians and three Turks are acquitted by a Rome court of conspiring to kill Pope John Paul. The court said it did not have enough proof to back prosecution charges that the six had backed Mehmet Ali Agca in his 1981 Rome assassination attempt. </P>
