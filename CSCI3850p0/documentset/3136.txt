<TITLE> Four killed in Karachi as toll hits 80</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Four killed in Karachi as toll hits 80</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PAKISTAN-VIOLENCE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KARACHI, Pakistan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Gunmen killed four more people, including a policeman, in Karachi, pushing the toll from mounting violence to 80 in 12 days, police said Monday. </P>
<P> Gunmen in a taxi shot and killed a 30-year-old soup vendor at his roadside stall Sunday night in an eastern district of the city. Police also found the body of a man with bullet wounds in the same area. </P>
<P> A betel-leaf trader was killed in a western part of Karachi by attackers on motorcycles who sprayed bullets at his shop. </P>
<P> A 32-year-old police wireless operator was ambushed and shot to death in the early hours of Sunday in an eastern area of the city. Police found his body lying outside his car. </P>
<P> More than 650 people have been killed this year in Karachi, where sectarian, ethnic and factional feuding has spun out of control. The violence has intensified since army troops withdrew on Nov. 30, ending their 29-month Operation Cleanup. </P>
<P> Newspapers quoted Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto as telling her parliamentary supporters Sunday the government was not considering imposing martial law on Sindh province, whose capital is Karachi, and would not redeploy the army there. </P>
<P> The government has yet to reveal how it plans to tackle the insecurity haunting Pakistan's biggest city, awash with guns and beset by conflicts between rival factions of the ethnic Mohajir National Movement (MQM), and Sunni and Shi'ite Muslim militants. </P>
