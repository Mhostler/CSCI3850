<TITLE> Elections to choose World Court set for June 21</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Elections to choose World Court set for June 21</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-UN-COURT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>UNITED NATIONS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Security Council decided without a vote Thursday to hold elections June 21 to fill the unexpired term of World Court Judge Roberto Ago of Italy, who died last month. </P>
<P> Ago first became a member of the Hague-based International Court of Justice in 1979 and was re-elected in 1988 to another nine-year term which would have expired Feb. 5, 1997. </P>
<P> The World Court has 15 judges who are chosen in elections held concurrent by the 15-nation Security Council and the 185-member General Assembly. </P>
