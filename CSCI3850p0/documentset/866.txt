<TITLE> CHRIS REMAINS A TROPICAL STORM</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> CHRIS REMAINS A TROPICAL STORM</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-WEATHER-USA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CORAL GABLES, Fla (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Tropical Storm Chris continued to move across the Atlantic Ocean but remained a tropical storm Thursday morning as forecasters reported the tempest had temporarily stopped gaining strength. </P>
<P> As of 11 a.m. EDT, the storm's center was located far from land, at a point about 800 miles east of the Lesser Antilles islands of the eastern Caribbean Sea, the National Hurricane Center said. </P>
<P> The storm was moving toward the west northwest near 14 miles per hour. ``This general motion is expected to continue through Friday,'' forecasters said. </P>
<P> They said the storm's intensification had temporarily stopped and maximum sustained winds remained near 70 miles per hour, where they had been since Wednesday evening. </P>
<P> But the National Hurricane Center warned that Chris might still become a hurricane during the next 24 hours. </P>
<P> Tropical Storm force winds extended outward up to 115 miles from the center. </P>
