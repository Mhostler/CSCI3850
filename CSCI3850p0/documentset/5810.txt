<TITLE> Two dead in Alaska air crash</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Two dead in Alaska air crash</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CRASH </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ANCHORAGE, Alaska (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two men were killed when their airplane crashed on a glacier in Alaska's Denali National Park at the weekend, the Park Service said Monday. </P>
<P> Pilot John Blosberg, 35, of Peters Creek, Alaska, and passenger Christopher Rosin, 34, of Trapper Creek, Alaska, were killed in the crash of their Cessna 180 airplane Saturday morning, National Park Service spokesman John Quinley said. </P>
<P> Searchers using a helicopter reached the wreckage the next day, after being turned back Saturday by bad weather. The plane crashed on the northeast fork of Eldridge Glacier, about 50 miles northwest of Talkeetna, Quinley said. </P>
