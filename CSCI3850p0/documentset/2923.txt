<TITLE> Quayle improving, doctors say</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Quayle improving, doctors say</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAYLE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>INDIANAPOLIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Former Vice President Dan Quayle was reported in improved condition Wednesday as treatment continued for a blood clot in one lung. </P>
<P> ``He's improving,'' said Pam Perry, spokeswoman for Indiana University Medical Center. </P>
<P> ``His color is back to normal. He's in good spirits. His doctors say he was up and moving about his room. And he's back on a normal diet,'' she added. </P>
<P> Quayle, 47, entered the hospital Monday suffering from a progressive shortness of breath. He was diagnosed as having a pulmonary embolism and is being treated with blood thinners and rest. </P>
<P> Doctors said they expect him to be released in about a week and to make a complete recovery. </P>
