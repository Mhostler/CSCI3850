<TITLE> Five killed by Russian bombing, Afghan radio says</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Five killed by Russian bombing, Afghan radio says</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AFGHAN-BOMBING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ISLAMABAD, Pakistan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Russian jets bombed an Afghan town close to the border with Tajikistan, killing at least five people, the official Kabul Radio said Tuesday. </P>
<P> The radio broadcast, monitored in Islamabad, said the jets dropped 19 bombs in the attack Monday on the town of Madessa in Badakhshan province. </P>
<P> Badakhshan borders Tajikistan, where thousands of Russian and Commonwealth of Independent States troops are deployed to help the government combat cross-border raids by Tajik Islamic rebels based in Afghanistan. </P>
<P> Clashes between the rebels and Russian border guards have intensified in recent days. </P>
<P> The radio quoted the Afghan Foreign Ministry as condemning the bombing, the second reported by Kabul within two days. </P>
<P> The radio said several people were killed Sunday when Russian jets bombed a district of the adjacent province of Takhar. </P>
<P> In recent months Afghanistan has complained several times of Russian air raids from Tajikistan. </P>
