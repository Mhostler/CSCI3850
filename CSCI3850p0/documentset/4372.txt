<TITLE> NHL-Rangers rally with two in the third to beat Buffalo</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> NHL-Rangers rally with two in the third to beat Buffalo</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NHL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BUFFALO, N.Y. (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Steve Larmer's goal with 63 seconds left to play capped a two-goal third-period rally as the New York Rangers, rounding into their championship form, beat the Buffalo Sabres 2-1 Wednesday. </P>
<P> Buffalo led 1-0 when Brian Noonan tied it up with with just 4:46 left in the game. </P>
<P> Larmer got the game-winner when he pounced on a rebound and backhanded it past Dominik Hasek for his second consecutive game-winning goal and sixth goal of the season. </P>
<P> ``I just happened to be in the right spot at the right time,'' Larmer said. </P>
<P> Newly acquired defenseman Alexei Zhitnik had the only goal through two periods. Zhitnik, acquired from Los Angeles Tuesday in a multi-player deal, tallied 8:45 into the game on his first shot in a Sabres uniform. </P>
<P> The Stanley Cup champion Rangers have begun to pick up their play after a slow start. New York is 4-1-1 in its last six games and pulled into a three-way tie for first in the Atlantic Division. </P>
<P> ``We work and get the right opportunity, no goalie is going to stop it,'' Rangers captain Mark Messier said. </P>
<P> Rangers goaltender Mike Richter stopped 25 shots. </P>
<P> Buffalo lost two defensemen in the game. Craig Muni pulled a hamstring and is expected to be out two to four weeks. Ken Sutton suffered a broken right index finger and is expected to be out for about three weeks. </P>
<P> In Dallas, Kelly Hrudey made 42 saves and Tony Granato snapped a 1-1 tie with a second-period goal and the Los Angeles Kings held on to defeat the Stars 3-1. </P>
<P> Over the last two periods, Hrudey did not allow a goal and made 28 saves. </P>
<P> John Druce and Pat Conacher scored the Kings' first and third goals. </P>
<P> Trent Klatt scored the Stars' lone goal. Dave Gagner picked up an assist for his 500th career point. </P>
<P> Dallas is 0-5-1 in its last six games including three straight losses at home. </P>

<P> Frantisek Kucera, who assisted on Sanderson's first two goals, scored the Whalers' fourth goal. </P>
<P> Newly acquired Mark Recchi had the only Montreal goal. </P>
<P> The victory helped struggling Hartford snap a six-game winless streak. Montreal has just two wins in its last nine games. </P>
<P> At New Jersey, Stephane Richer scored two goals and assisted on another as the Devils beat the Washington Capitals 4-2, giving coach Jacques Lemaire his 100th career victory. </P>
<P> New Jersey also got goals from Bob Carpenter and Randy McKay. Dave Poulin and Michal Pivonka tallied for the Caps. </P>
<P> New Jersey goalie Martin Brodeur made 20 saves, 10 of them in the third period. </P>
<P> Washington, winless in its last five games (0-4-1), has a league-low 22 goals this season. </P>
<P> In Winnipeg, Keith Primeau scored two goals and set up another and Chris Osgood stopped 30 shots to lead the Detroit Red Wings to a 5-1 victory over the Jets. </P>
<P> Sergei Fedorov added a goal and an assist for the Red Wings, who won at Winnipeg for the first time since 1990. </P>
<P> Alexei Zhamnov scored the only goal for the Jets, who had a two-game winning streak snapped. </P>
<P> At Florida, Mark Fitzpatrick made 21 saves for his fifth career shutout as the Panthers beat the struggling Ottawa Senators 2-0. </P>
<P> Jesse Belanger and Stu Barnes scored third-period goals for Florida, which won for just the third time in its last eight games (3-4-1). Fitzpatrick was barely tested in the final two periods, stopping just eight shots. </P>
<P> Don Beaupre made 28 saves for the Senators, who have lost seven of their last eight. </P>
<P> In Toronto, Jason Arnott scored two goals and assisted on another and Bill Ranford made 27 saves as the Edmonton Oilers ran their unbeaten streak to six games with a 4-1 victory over the Maple Leafs. </P>
<P> Kelly Buchberger and Scott Thornton also tallied for Edmonton. Dave Ellett scored for Toronto. </P>
<P> In San Jose, Sergei Makarov had a goal and two assists and Wade Flaherty made 36 saves as the Sharks defeated the Vancouver Canucks 3-1 to snap a five-game winless streak. </P>
<P> Igor Larionov added a goal and and an assist for the Sharks. Ray Whitney scored San Jose's first goal. </P>
<P> Trevor Linden broke up Flaherty's bid for his first career shutout on the power play with just under two minutes remaining in the game. </P>
