<TITLE> Professor who escaped concentration camp dead</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Professor who escaped concentration camp dead</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EHRMANN-OBITUARY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>HANOVER, N.H. (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Henry W. Ehrmann, a political science professor at Dartmouth College who had been a prisoner in a German concentration camp, died Christmas Day in La Jolla, Calif, at the age of 86, Dartmouth College said Tuesday. </P>
<P> Ehrmann, born in Berlin, earned a law degree at the University of Freiburg and a doctorate in jurisprudence at the University of Berlin. </P>
<P> He was arrested shortly after the Nazis took power in 1933 and imprisoned in a concentration camp. With the help of friends, and through bribes, Ehrmann escaped Germany by skiing over the Sudeten Mountains into Czechoslovakia, Dartmouth said. </P>
<P> Ehrmann and his wife, Claire, escaped the Nazi occupation of France in 1940 by crossing the Pyrenees Mountains to Spain, and eventually, the United States. Ehrmann married Claire in France in 1939. </P>
<P> In 1961, Ehrmann joined the Dartmouth Government Department faculty and was department chairman from 1963-66. </P>
<P> He also taught at McGill University in Montreal, the Universities of Paris, Bordeaux, Grenoble, Mannheim, Berlin, as well as Princeton University and University of California campuses at Berkeley and San Diego. </P>
<P> He is survived by his wife and two sons, Michael M. of McLean, Va., and Paul L. of Santa Monica, Calif. </P>
<P> Memorial services will be held in the spring. </P>
