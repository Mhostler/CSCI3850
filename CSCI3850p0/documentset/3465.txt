<SUBJECT> BC-CIS-CHECHNYA-ADVANCE -2 MOSCOW </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> ``Today Russian armed forces, including interior and defense ministry units, are carrying out real actions to move toward the outskirts of Grozny,'' the government press service quoted an official at the Russian military base commanding the operation in Mozdok, just outside Chechnya, as saying. </P>
<P> ``They are carrying out the task of closing and tightening the circle around the Chechen capital, where a large number of illegal armed formations have assembled, including well-trained volunteers,'' it said. </P>
<P> The government press service did not specify what role the air force had been playing Wednesday and could not confirm that the Grozny area had been bombed. </P>
<P> Interfax quoted an armed forces representative in Mozdok as saying separately that jets had bombed Chechen units in woods along road and bridges. It said artillery and bomb strikes were launched in the town of Argun and nearby Petropavlovskaya. </P>
