<TITLE> Colombian gunmen kidnap local Lions Club head</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Colombian gunmen kidnap local Lions Club head</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-COLOMBIA-KIDNAPPING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BOGOTA, Colombia (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Unidentified gunmen kidnapped the host of a popular Colombian television show and a local representative of an international charity organization Sunday, a police spokesman said. </P>
<P> After setting up a road block near the town of Florida, more than 125 miles southwest of Bogota, gunmen took hostage Miguel Sandoval, a Lions Club representative, and Alfonso Lizarazo, comedian and host of the weekly show Sabado Felices (Happy Saturdays), the spokesman said. </P>
<P> Sandoval and Lizarazo were driving with their assistants to nearby Cali after holding a fund-raising event in Florida when they were forced to stop at the road block and taken hostage, he said. </P>
<P> The spokesman said a front of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC), the largest guerrilla group in the country, was suspected. </P>
<P> Lizarazo performs in a different towns every week to raise money for various causes. Sandoval, who leads the Lions Club in Valle del Cauca province, had apparently invited Lizarazo to hold a show in Florida to help raise money for housing. </P>
