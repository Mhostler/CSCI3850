<TITLE> SOUTH AFRICAN PULITZER WINNER FOUND DEAD IN CAR</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> SOUTH AFRICAN PULITZER WINNER FOUND DEAD IN CAR</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SAFRICA-CARTER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>JOHANNESBURG, South Africa (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Pulitzer Prize-winning South African news photographer Kevin Patrick Carter, 33, was found dead in his car on Wednesday night, police said Thursday. </P>
<P> Carter won the 1993 Pulitzer Prize for feature photography with a chilling picture of a vulture standing by a starving child in Sudan. </P>
<P> The Pulitzers are the highest U.S. awards in journalism and the arts. </P>
<P> A police spokesman said two patrolling policemen had found Carter's body in his car. An inquest indicated he had died of carbon monoxide poisoning, the spokesman said. </P>
<P> He said Carter had left a number of letters and no foul play was suspected. </P>
<P> Carter had a contract with the Paris-based Sygma photographic agency at the time of his death. </P>
