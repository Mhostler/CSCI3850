<TITLE> JAPAN'S MAIN ISLAND JOLTED BY TWO TREMORS</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> JAPAN'S MAIN ISLAND JOLTED BY TWO TREMORS</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-JAPAN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TOKYO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two moderate earthquakes shook the northern part of Japan's main island of Honshu Tuesday evening, the Meteorological Office said. </P>
<P> It said the first quake, measuring 6.2 on the Richter scale, struck at 7:09 p.m. (local time) and the second at 7:18 p.m. The tremors were felt in Tokyo, where office buildings swayed slightly. </P>
<P> The second earthquake measured 5.0 on the Richter scale. </P>
<P> Meteorological officials said there was no danger of ``tsunami'' or tidal waves from either of the tremors, both of which occurred deep in the Pacific Ocean. </P>
<P> There were no reports of damage or injuries. </P>
<P> The epicenter of the first earthquake was 19 miles below the seabed, said a meteorological official. </P>
