<TITLE> Simpson very upset at news of wife's death - officer</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Simpson very upset at news of wife's death - officer</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SIMPSON-REACTION </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LOS ANGELES (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  O.J. Simpson's first words on being told his ex-wife had been killed were, ``Oh my God, Nicole is killed. Oh my God, she's dead,'' a detective testified Thursday at the football legend's double murder trial. </P>
<P> Detective Ron Phillips said he called Simpson at his Chicago Hotel the day after Nicole Brown Simpson and her friend Ronald Goldman were murdered. </P>
<P> Phillips said he told Simpson that he had some bad news for him, that Nicole had been killed. </P>
<P> He said Simpson became ``very upset at the news. ``I kept trying to calm him down and he continued to be upset, which I expected,'' Phillips said. </P>
<P> Simpson has pleaded not guilty to the murders. </P>
