<TITLE> Chinese buy 30 million bicycles in 94, more in 95</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Chinese buy 30 million bicycles in 94, more in 95</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-BICYCLE (PICTURE) </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BEIJING (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Chinese bought 30.5 million bicycles in 1994 and demand in the country where the two-wheeled pedaller is king is expected to rise to 31 million next year, the Economic Information Daily said. </P>
<P> ``Our country is not only a giant kingdom for production of bicycles but is also the biggest market for bicycles in the world,'' said the newspaper, received in Beijing Monday. </P>
<P> Demand and output in 1994 exceeded the 1993 level, with estimates of domestic demand for the year at 30.5 million and forecast to rise to 31 million in 1995, it said. </P>
<P> China has become an important world exporter of bicycles in recent years, with overseas sales soaring in recent years -- to 10.2 million in 1992 from 2.44 million in 1989, it said. </P>
<P> The newspaper estimated annual world demand at 20 million. </P>
<P> Exports in 1995 are forecast to rise to 10.5 million, it said. </P>
<P> China has 800 bicycle-related industries, of which 200 manufacture finished bicycles and 600 produce bicycle parts, it said. </P>
<P> At the end of the Seventh Five Year plan, which finishes at the end of this year, Chinese owned 390 million bicycles -- or 34.2 bicycles for every 100 people, it said. </P>
<P> An ordinary bicycle in China now costs between $41 and $65. </P>
