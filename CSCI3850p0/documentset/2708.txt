<TITLE> Cowboys fined $10,000 for hiding Aikman injury</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Cowboys fined $10,000 for hiding Aikman injury</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NFL-COWBOYS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The National Football League Friday fined the Dallas Cowboys $10,000 for violating league injury report procedures last week regarding a thumb ailment to star quarterback Troy Aikman. </P>
<P> NFL Commissioner Paul Tagliabue fined the Cowboys for failing to follow proper procedure last Friday after Aikman suffered a thumb injury during practice. </P>
<P> NFL rules require teams to supply information on all injured players Wednesday and Thursday and to report additional injuries after Thursday to the league office, the news media, the opposing team and the network televising their game. </P>
<P> The policy instructs teams to pay particular attention to key players such as quarterbacks. </P>
<P> Tagliabue said the Cowboys failed to follow the procedure with the injury to Aikman, which was serious enough to require an x-ray and treatment. </P>
<P> Aikman played Sunday and threw three interceptions as the Cowboys lost to the San Francisco 49ers 21-14. </P>
