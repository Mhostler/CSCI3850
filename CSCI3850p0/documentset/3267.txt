<SUBJECT> BC-DRUGS-PROBE-USA -2 WASHINGTON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> U.S. officials lauded the work of counterparts in Italy, Spain, Canada and Britain, and said they were ready to use the same kind of border-spanning tactics, including use of a bank as a front, to catch more criminals. </P>
<P> Thomas Constantine, head of the U.S. Drug Enforcement Agency, estimateded $200 million was laundered through a bank in the British West Indies that was used by investigators to gather information about where drug money came from and how it was spent. </P>
<P> The investigation began when DEA agents were ``hired'' by Colombian traffickers to run money pickups in the United States. The investigation also showed the Italian crime organization of Pasquale Locatelli was transporting Colombian cocaine to Europe. </P>
