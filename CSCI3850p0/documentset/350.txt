<TITLE> GAMBIAN TROOPS RIOT, AIRPORT CLOSED - WITNESSES</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> GAMBIAN TROOPS RIOT, AIRPORT CLOSED - WITNESSES</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-GAMBIA-MUTINY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>DAKAR, Senegal (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Gambian infantrymen protesting over unpaid wages rampaged through the capital Banjul Friday, seizing cars and looting shops, witnesses said. </P>
<P> The airport in Banjul was closed and some public buildings were occupied by mutineers but the witnesses, arriving overland in neighboring Senegal, did not report any shooting. </P>
<P> Gambia's 800-strong army is led by a Nigerian colonel under a 1992 agreement between the two former British colonies. Banjul could not be reached by telephone and the city's public telex office appeared to have been abandoned. </P>
