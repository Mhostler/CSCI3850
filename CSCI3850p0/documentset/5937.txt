<TITLE> Suspect in Oklahoma bombing charged</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Suspect in Oklahoma bombing charged</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BLAST-MCVEIGH-CHARGE 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>  (Adds detsils)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Timothy James McVeigh, 27, was formally charged on Friday with the bombing of a federal building on Oklahoma City which killed at least 65 people, the Justice Department said. </P>
<P> McVeigh was arrested earlier on Friday in Perry, Oklahoma, and transported by military helicopter and under heavy guard to Tinker Air Force Base outside Oklahoma City to be formally charged.. </P>
<P> The criminal complaint against him, released by the Justice Department, said that on April 19, McVeigh ``did maliciously damage and destroy by means of fire or an explosive, any building, vehicle and other personal or real property in whole or in part owned, possessed, or used by the United States, any department or agency thereof'' in violation of the law. </P>
<P> A Department spokesman said the charge carried the death penalty. </P>
