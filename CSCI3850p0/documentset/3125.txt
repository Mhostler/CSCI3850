<TITLE> Americas Summit sets free trade timetable</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Americas Summit sets free trade timetable</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AMERICAS-TIMETABLE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MIAMI (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Leaders from 34 Western Hemisphere nations have agreed on a timetable for creating what would be the world's biggest trade bloc, the Free Trade Area of the Americas, according to a final communique to be released on Sunday. </P>
<P> At the same time, the members of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) -- Canada, the United States and Mexico -- are inviting Chile to start negotiations on joining. Following are key dates in the two processes: </P>
<P> January 1995 - Committees start considering how existing regional trade agreements might be brought together. </P>
<P> April 1995 - Chile expects President Clinton will have secured ``fast-track'' negotiating authority from Congress, allowing formal negotiations on its NAFTA entry to start. </P>
<P> June 1995 - Trade ministers from the Americas meet to consider first reports from study committees. </P>
<P> March 1996 - Final meeting of trade ministers to set timetable for further progress towards free trade area. </P>
<P> Mid-1996 - Chile expects to become full member of NAFTA. </P>
