<TITLE> Chinese girl kidnapped, raped, forced to marry</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Chinese girl kidnapped, raped, forced to marry</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-RAPE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SHANGHAI, March 25 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A 17-year-old Chinese schoolgirl was kidnapped, beaten and raped by a peasant and forcibly married to him for more than two years, the Wen Hui Bao newspaper reported on Saturday. </P>
<P> In November 1992, Yan Yue, then 17, was doing her internship at a hotel in Jinzhou, Liaoning province in northeast China when she was stopped near the local railway station by a man with a knife, it said. </P>
<P> The man, a peasant, forced her to go the house of his uncle and aunt, both in the police, despite her screams and protests, it said. </P>
<P> At this house, he raped her and kept her locked up, beating her whenever she tried to escape, while his uncle and aunt paid no attention. </P>
<P> In October 1993, the peasant arranged their marriage by presenting photographs and other documents and attending the ceremony himself, but without her. </P>
<P> Yan's family spent 30,000 yuan ($3,570) in their efforts to find their daughter. On January 21 this year, her father was in the village where she was detained and spotted her as she came out of a public toilet. </P>
<P> She jumped into his car and escaped, the newspaper said. </P>
