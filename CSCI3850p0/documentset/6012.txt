<TITLE> Non-Aligned ministers open three-day meeting</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Non-Aligned ministers open three-day meeting</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-INDONESIA-NAM-OPENING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BANDUNG, Indonesia, April 25 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Foreign ministers and officials of the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) opened a three-day meeting on Rwanda, the United Nations and other issues in the Indonesian town of Bandung on Tuesday. </P>
<P> ``Our movement has taken concrete action to address the challenges of the post-Cold War era,'' Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas said. </P>
<P> ``The important thing is to forge ahead, maintaining the momentum that we have thus far generated,'' he told the opening session of the coordinating ministerial meeting, attended by ministers and representatives from 97 of NAM's 111 members. </P>
<P> Indonesia has led the movement for the past three years. It is scheduled to hand over to Colombia in mid-1995. </P>
<P> The agenda for the meeting in Bandung, West Java, includes discussions on the nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT), the crisis in Rwanda and restructuring the United Nations. </P>
