<TITLE> Indonesia to send sappers battalion to Bosnia</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Indonesia to send sappers battalion to Bosnia</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-INDONESIA-BOSNIA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>JAKARTA, March 31 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Indonesia will send a sappers' battalion to the war-torn former Yugoslavia, the official Antara news agency said on Friday. </P>
<P> It quoted Coordinating Minister for Political and Security Affairs Susilo Sudarman as saying the 441-strong battalion will be sent to Bosnia to help in minesweeping operations and programmes to rehabilitate residential areas. </P>
<P> ``Preparations for the departure of the sappers' battalion are still under way,'' he was quoted as saying. He did not say when it will leave. </P>
<P> Susilo also said the 236-strong Indonesian medical team currently in Bosnia will be replaced by a new contigent. </P>
<P> The United States earlier this month welcomed Indonesian initiatives to help bring peace to the former Yugoslavia. </P>
<P> Mainly Moslem Indonesia and Moslem nations have backed the Bosnian government's quest to recover land taken over by Serbs. </P>
