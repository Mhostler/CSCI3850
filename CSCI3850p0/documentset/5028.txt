<TITLE> Corsica hit by 35 small bomb blasts, three injured</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Corsica hit by 35 small bomb blasts, three injured</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FRANCE-CORSICA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>AJACCIO, Corsica (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Thirty-five small bombs exploded Sunday night at public buildings in the French Mediterranean island of Corsica, where separatists want autonomy, police sources said. </P>
<P> Three people were slightly injured and the blasts shattered windows and caused other minor damage. </P>
<P> There were no immediate claims of responsibility. </P>
<P> The blasts, before midnight and across the island including nine in Ajaccio in southwest Corsica, hit treasury offices, border police offices, a tax building and six branches of state-owned bank Credit Lyonnais. </P>
<P> Corsica has been racked by strikes in recent weeks. </P>
<P> The main separatist group, the National Front for the Liberation of Corsica, said in November it was prepared to negotiate a compromise with Paris and was suspending attacks on civil servants in mainland France and French residents of Corsica. </P>
