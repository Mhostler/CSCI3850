<TITLE> Kids were playing on radioactive cannon - paper</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Kids were playing on radioactive cannon - paper</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-RUSSIA-CANNON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  An artillery piece used as a children's climbing frame in a Moscow park was beaming out dangerous levels of radiation, Komsomolskaya Pravda newspaper said Tuesday. </P>
<P> The paper said experts examining the naval cannon, in Moscow's Victory Park, discovered a night-vision device which was emitting radiation well above any permissible level. </P>
<P> The device was hurriedly removed, the paper said. </P>
<P> Victory Park was inaugurated last summer and displays one of the biggest collections of military armor and weaponry in Russia. Children are allowed to scramble on the equipment and the newspaper said the cannon had been a particular favorite. </P>
