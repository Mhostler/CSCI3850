<TITLE> Two small earthquakes felt in northern California</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Two small earthquakes felt in northern California</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-CALIFORNIA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SAN FRANCISCO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two small earthquakes shook northern California Tuesday but there were no reports of damage or injuries, the U.S. Geological Survey said. </P>
<P> The first quake, measuring 3.8 on the Richter scale, struck in the early evening and was followed minutes later by a 3.1 tremor, said Lu Page, a spokeswoman for the Geological Survey, which monitors earthquakes. </P>
<P> The quakes on the Calaveras Fault were centered 11 miles east of San Jose. The rolling tremor was widely felt in the city, but there were no reports of damage or injuries. </P>
