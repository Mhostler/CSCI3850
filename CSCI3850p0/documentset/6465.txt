<TITLE> Wives of jailed Americans leave Baghdad</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Wives of jailed Americans leave Baghdad</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-IRAQ-AMERICANS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BAGHDAD (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The wives of two Americans jailed in Iraq for illegal entry left Baghdad Thursday after a last visit to their husbands in prison. </P>
<P> Linda Barloon and Kathy Daliberti declined to speak to reporters as they set out on the 12-hour drive across the desert to the Jordanian capital Amman. </P>
<P> U.N. sanctions imposed for Iraqs 1990 invasion of Kuwait have closed Iraqi air space. </P>
<P> William Barloon, 39, and David Daliberti, 41, were arrested in Iraq on March 13 after crossing the border from Kuwait. They were tried and sentenced to eight years in jail for illegally entering Iraq. </P>
<P> Their wives arrived in Baghdad on April 29 with the intention of asking Iraqi President Saddam Hussein for clemency. </P>
<P> They first met their husbands in Abu Ghraib prison outside Baghdad on April 30 and both said that, given the circumstances, they looked fine. </P>
