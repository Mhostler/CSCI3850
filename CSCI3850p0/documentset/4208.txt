<TITLE> NHL-League suspends Luc Robitaille</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> NHL-League suspends Luc Robitaille</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NHL-ROBITAILLE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Pittsburgh Penguins left wing Luc Robitaille was suspended indefinitely by the National Hockey League Tuesday for a high-sticking incident against the New Jersey Devils on Feb. 5. </P>
<P> NHL Senior Vice President and Director of Hockey Operations Brian Burke announced that Robitaille is suspended pending further review of the incident and a hearing in New York on Wednesday. That meant he would miss Tuesday night's game against the Florida Panthers. </P>
<P> The incident under review came in overtime of a 3-3 tie with the Devils. Robitaille received a minor penalty for high-sticking New Jersey defenseman Bruce Driver. </P>
<P> Robitaille, in his first season with the Penguins after eight seasons with the Los Angeles Kings, has eight points on three goals and five assists in eight games this season. </P>
<P> The 29-year-old Robitaille has 395 goals and 416 assists for 811 points during his career. </P>
