<TITLE> SENATE TO FORCE CRIME VOTE, THEN ADJOURN</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> SENATE TO FORCE CRIME VOTE, THEN ADJOURN</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-CRIME-CONGRESS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Senate Majority Leader George Mitchell said he hoped to force a Senate vote on the controversial crime bill Thursday afternoon and then adjourn the Senate until ``after Labor Day.''  The first vote would be a procedural one involving a Republican objection, technically known as a ``point of order'' that would determine the fate of the bill. </P>
