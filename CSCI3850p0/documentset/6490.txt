<TITLE> Motor Racing-Brayton breaks track record in Indy 500 practice</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Motor Racing-Brayton breaks track record in Indy 500 practice</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MOTOR RACING-INDY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>INDIANAPOLIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  American Scott Brayton set an unofficial track record at the Indianapolis Motor Speedway on Thursday in a practice session for the Indianapolis 500. </P>
<P> Brayton, driving one of the powerful Lola-Menards, was clocked at 234.656 mile per hour at ``the old brickyard.'' </P>
<P> Brayton set the mark only minutes after teammate Arie Luyendyk of the Netherlands had broken his own unofficial record by going 234.332 mph. The Dutchman had set the previous mark of 234.107 mph in practice on Monday. </P>
<P> Eight drivers exceeded 230 mph as final preparations were being made for Saturday's run for the pole position on the starting grid for the May 28 race. </P>
<P> Canadian Scott Gooodyear, driving a Reynard powerered by a revamped Honda engine, was third fastest at 232.246 mph. </P>
<P> Goodyear's Honda teammate Andre Ribeiro of Brazil became the first driver ever to record 230 mph laps in two differnt cars on the same day. </P>
<P> Rebeiro had laps of 231.523 in his primary car and 230.144 in his backup ride. </P>
