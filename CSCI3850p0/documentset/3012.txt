<TITLE> Jacob Kaplan, former chief rabbi of France, dies</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Jacob Kaplan, former chief rabbi of France, dies</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FRANCE-RABBI </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>PARIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Jacob Kaplan, a former chief rabbi of France, died Monday from a lung ailment, Jewish community officials said. He was 99. </P>
<P> Kaplan served as chief rabbi from 1955 to 1980. </P>
<P> He attracted public attention in 1967 when he publicly criticized President Charles de Gaulle, who after the six-day Middle East War that year referred to the victorious Israelis as ``domineering'' and self-centered. </P>
