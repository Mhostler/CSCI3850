<TITLE> U.S. commander delivers regrets to N. Korea leader</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.S. commander delivers regrets to N. Korea leader</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-HELICOPTER-LETTER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SEOUL, South Korea (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The U.S. military commander in South Korea sent a letter Saturday to North Korean leader Kim Jong-il, expressing regret at the intrusion by a U.S. Army helicopter a week ago, a U.S. military source said. </P>
<P> The letter from Gen. Gary Luck was delivered to the North at the Korean border where the U.S. military was trying to arrange talks for the release of the helicopter's pilot Bobby Hall, he said. </P>
<P> Luck promised measures to prevent similar intrusions in the future and urged Kim to help speed up the release of Hall who was captured by North Korea after his aircraft strayed north across the tense Korean border, the source said. </P>
<P> ``General Luck expressed regrets at this unfortunate incident and pledged measures to prevent similar incidents in the future,'' he said. </P>
<P> The general also thanked North Korea for returning the body Thursday of Hall's co-pilot, who was killed in the downing. </P>
<P> Washington has asked North Korea to free Hall by Christmas. </P>
