<TITLE> Two dead in Egyptian mosque attack, police ambush</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Two dead in Egyptian mosque attack, police ambush</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-MILITANTS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAIRO, Egypt (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Suspected Muslim militants stormed a mosque near the southern Egyptian town of Mallawi Saturday, killing a government employee and wounding another man, the Interior Ministry said in a statement. </P>
<P> It said three gunmen broke into Zawiyya mosque in Tanda, once run by sympathizers of the militant Gama'a al-Islamiya (Islamic Group) but recently taken over by the Ministry of Religious Affairs. </P>
<P> They opened fire, killing the mosque keeper and wounding another man before fleeing, the statement said. </P>
<P> In a separate incident, the ministry said police killed a Muslim militant early Saturday after ambushing him on one of the roads leading out of Mallawi. </P>
<P> It said the man opened fire on police after being stopped and was killed in the exchange of fire. </P>
<P> Five hundred people have been killed in militant-related violence in Egypt since the Gama'a, which says it is committed to overthrowing the government of President Hosni Mubarak, began attacking government officials, Egyptian Christians and foreign tourists nearly three years ago. </P>
