<TITLE> Pakistani separatist politician dies</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pakistani separatist politician dies</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PAKISTAN-SYED </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KARACHI, Pakistan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Pakistani separatist politician G.M. Syed died Tuesday after a protracted illness, family members said. He was 92. </P>
<P> Syed, the chief of Jiye Sindh movement, died in a Karachi hospital and will be buried at his home village of Sann in southern province of Sindh Wednesday, the family said. </P>
<P> Syed, Pakistan's oldest well-known politician campaigned for a separate ``Sindhudesh'' homeland in the volatile Sindh province, of which the industrial port city of Karachi is the capital. </P>
<P> He was the author of 45 books, some of which remain unpublished and some banned by authorities for his separatist views. </P>
<P> The Jiye Sindh movement had a strong following in the Sindh countryside as well as among the intelligentsia, but never gained enough electoral support to win an election. </P>
