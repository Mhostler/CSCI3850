<P> ``The rest of the world is looking at us,'' Clinton said. ''We have a golden opportunity here ... to fulfill our two responsibilities: our responsibility to lead and remain engaged in the world and our responsibility to try to help the people here at home to get ahead. We need to get on with it and do it now.''  The Democratic president spoke to an assemblage packed with Cabinet officers and economic advisers of previous administrations including such Republican luminaries as former Secretary of State James Baker and former Reagan White House Budget Director James Miller. Earlier the White House released a letter from former Republican Presidents George Bush and Gerald Ford and Democrat Jimmy Carter supporting the pact. </P>
<P> The House of Representatives was scheduled to vote on the accord on Tuesday and the Senate on Thursday. Prospects for passage looked good. </P>
