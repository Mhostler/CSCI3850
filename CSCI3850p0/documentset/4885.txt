<TITLE> China vows to enhance copyright protection</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> China vows to enhance copyright protection</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-COPYRIGHT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BEIJING (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  China's top judge said Monday the courts jailed 341 people for trademark infringement in 1994 and will intensify their battle against piracy this year. </P>
<P> ``China has redoubled efforts in the protection of intellectual property rights in recent years and marked progress was achieved in legislation and law enforcement in this field,'' Ren Jianxin, president of the Supreme People's Court, told parliament. </P>
<P> Chinese courts jailed the 341 for involvement in 411 cases of trademark infringement in 1994, he said. </P>
<P> The courts handled 1,662 cases of trademark, patent and copyright infringement last year. He gave no comparative figures. </P>
<P> In all cases, the parties concerned -- whether Chinese or foreign -- were treated equally and without discrimination, he said. </P>
<P> Saturday, China and the United States signed a landmark accord to protect copyrights and open China's entertainment market to U.S. exporters. </P>
<P> The accord, reached last month to halt rampant piracy of intellectual property defused a looming trade war. </P>
<P> The Supreme Court will set up a special tribunal to try intellectual property rights disputes, Ren said. </P>
<P> In addition to Beijing and southern Guangdong province, several cities have established special courts to handle intellectual property rights cases, he said. </P>
