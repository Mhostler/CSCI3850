<P> Clinton restated his opposition to the amendment but in recognition of its popularity said, ``I support the impulses that are giving rise to it.'' </P>
<P> But he said compliance with the amendment runs the risk of ''turning recessions into near depressions'' and that America's persistent problem of deficit spending can be fixed without an amendment. </P>
<P> ``What happens when we have a recession? Are we going to be raising taxes to pay for job-training programs?... I'm just afraid the American people have not been told the full implications of this for social security, for education and the economy,'' he told reporters. </P>
