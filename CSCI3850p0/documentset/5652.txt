<TITLE> Gunmen kill Russian banker in center of Moscow</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Gunmen kill Russian banker in center of Moscow</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-RUSSIA-BANKER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A 33-year-old Russian banker was shot dead on a central Moscow avenue Monday night by two unidentified gunmen who opened fire on his BMW, police said on Tuesday. </P>
<P> In an attack bearing the hallmarks of a gangland killing, Vadim Yafyasov, vice-president of Russia's commercial Yugorsky Bank, and his bodyguard were killed as their car was driving along Kutuzovsky Avenue, police said. </P>
<P> Yafyasov's driver was hospitalized with injuries. </P>
<P> Police gave no details of possible motives for the murder. Yugorsky Bank is based in Nizhnyevartovsk in the Siberian oil region of Tyumen and has close contacts with the oil and energy industry. </P>
<P> The killing is the latest in a series of murders which include the gunning down of television journalist Vladislav Listyev and the blowing up of investigative reporter Dmitry Kholodov by a briefcase bomb. </P>
<P> None of the murderers have been found. </P>
<P> Organized crime has soared in Russia since the 1991 collapse of the Soviet Union. </P>
<P> Parliamentary deputy and businessman Sergei Skorochkin was found shot dead in February after being kidnapped. Two other deputies have been murdered since elections to the State Duma lower chamber in December 1993. </P>
