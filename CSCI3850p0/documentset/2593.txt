<TITLE> U.S., S. Korea and Japan leaders to meet</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.S., S. Korea and Japan leaders to meet</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-APEC-KOREA-MEETING URGENT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>JAKARTA, Indonesia (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  President Clinton, South Korean President Kim Young-sam and Japanese Prime Minister Tomiichi Murayama will meet Monday in Jakarta to discuss the nuclear accord Washington signed with North Korea, a U.S. official said. </P>
<P> The official told Reuters the three leaders wanted to give public attention and momentum to the nuclear agreement in advance of talks next week at expert level on the structure and financing of alternative energy sources for Pyongyang that are critical to the accord's success. </P>
<P> The three men are among the leaders gathered in Indonesia for the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum summit. </P>
