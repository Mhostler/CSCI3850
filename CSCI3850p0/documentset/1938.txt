<SUBJECT> BC-IRAQ-USA-SHALIKASHVILI -2 WASHINGTON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> ``The latest information we have now indicates that there is fairly broad movement in most of those (Iraqi) units that had been brought down south,'' General John Shalikashvili, chairman of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, told reporters. ``However, having said that, there is still an indication of considerable units still remaining ... </P>
<P> ``We do not have any indication as to where the units are preparing to move. And so we are continuing to watch the situation very carefully and at the same time we are continuing with the (U.S. military) deployments,'' Shalikashvili said. </P>
<P> He commented after reports from Iraq had said the promised Iraqi withdrawal was complete. </P>
<P> Shalikashvili added: ``We still have quite some time to look at this situation before we're certain as to what their intentions are and where they are moving. I'm not prepared yet to say that the crisis is over in any way.'' </P>
