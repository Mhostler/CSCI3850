<TITLE> Prince Philip to visit mother's Jerusalem grave</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Prince Philip to visit mother's Jerusalem grave</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-ISRAEL-ROYALS (PICTURE) </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TEL AVIV (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Britain's Prince Philip arrived in Israel Sunday to visit his mother's grave for the first time and receive a medal honoring her for saving a Jewish family from the Nazis in World War II. </P>
<P> The husband of Queen Elizabeth II arrived on a royal family executive jet at the start of a 25-hour private visit. </P>
<P> ``Although this is a private visit, it is very important and certainly an indication of a much better and improving relations between Israel and Britain,'' Israeli Education Minister Amnon Rubinstein told reporters. </P>
<P> Israel's national Holocaust memorial Yad Vashem will on Monday honor Prince Philip's mother Princess Alice for rescuing a Greek Jewish family from the Holocaust in which six million Jews were killed by the Nazis. </P>
<P> Princess Alice died in 1972 and was buried at her request on Jerusalem's Mount of Olives. Yad Vashem said the prince would visit her grave for the first time during his visit. </P>
