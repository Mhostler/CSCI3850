<TITLE> Zvereva may be disciplined for showing underwear</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Zvereva may be disciplined for showing underwear</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-OPEN-BRASSIERE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MELBOURNE, Australia (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Top women's doubles seed Natalia Zvereva may be disciplined for raising her shirt and showing her brassiere in a victory celebration at the Australian Open on Tuesday. </P>
<P> The 23-year-old Belarussian allegedly turned to the crown near Court One and lifted the front of her tennis shirt to reveal her brassiere after her quarter-final triumph in the women's doubles Tuesday. </P>
<P> A spokeswoman for the Women's Tennis Federation (WTA) said Zvereva would face a charge of unprofessional conduct after a courtside official lodged a complaint. </P>
<P> ``The matter will be referred to a disciplinary committee which will meet within the next 60 to 90 days,'' a WTA spokeswoman said. </P>
<P> Zvereva, a WTA Tour Players committee member, partnered American Gigi Fernandez to victory over tenth seeds Eugenia Maniokova of Russia and Leila Meshki of Georgia to reach the semi-finals at Flinders Park. </P>
