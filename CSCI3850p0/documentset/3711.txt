<TITLE> Aziz denies reports of Iraqi contacts with Israel</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Aziz denies reports of Iraqi contacts with Israel</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-IRAQ-UN-ISRAEL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>UNITED NATIONS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Iraqi deputy Prime Minister Tareq Aziz Wednesday denied reports of possible contacts between Iraq and Israel. </P>
<P> ``These reports are not correct,'' he said without elaborating. </P>
<P> Aziz was replying to a question from a reporter during a visit to the United Nations for a meeting with Security Council President Emilio Cardenas of Argentina in advance of a council review of Gulf War sanctions against Iraq. </P>
<P> An Israeli newspaper reported Monday that Iraq, which fired nearly 40 Scud missiles at Israel during the Gulf War, was ready to recognise the Jewish state and had asked Morocco to mediate between them. </P>
<P> Iraqi and Israeli officials have denied previous reports of such contacts. </P>
