<TITLE> First woman shuttle pilot an ace aviator</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> First woman shuttle pilot an ace aviator</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SPACE-SHUTTLE-COLLINS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAPE CANAVERAL, Fla (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Here are the facts about Air Force Lt. Col. Eileen Marie Collins, pilot of the U.S. space shuttle Discovery: </P>
<P> Born: Nov. 19, 1956 in Elmira, N.Y. </P>
<P> Parents: James and Rose Marie Collins. </P>
<P> Education: Bachelor of arts degree, mathematics and economics, Syracuse University, 1978; master of science degree, operations research, Stanford University, 1986; master of arts degree, space systems management, Webster University, 1989. </P>
<P> Married to Pat Youngs, a pilot for Delta Airlines. </P>
<P> Hobbies: Running, golf, hiking, camping, reading, photography and astronomy. </P>
<P> Experience: Test pilot with more than 4,000 hours in 30 different types of aircraft. </P>
