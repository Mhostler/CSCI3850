<TITLE> CHURCH FIRES VICAR WHO DOES NOT BELIEVE IN GOD</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> CHURCH FIRES VICAR WHO DOES NOT BELIEVE IN GOD</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BRITAIN-VICAR </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LONDON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Church of England has dismissed a vicar who does not believe in God, sparking angry protests from 65 of his fellow clergymen who said Anglican authorities should be more tolerant. </P>
<P> Reverend Anthony Freeman, 48, was dismissed by his local bishop after writing in a book: ``There is nothing out there -- or if there is, we can have no knowledge of it.'' </P>
<P> Sixty-five clerics criticized the decision by the Bishop of Chichester, Dr. Eric Kemp, saying: ``It reverses a long Church of England tradition that tolerates and values a wide range of views.'' </P>
<P> In a joint letter to the Independent newspaper Thursday, they said: ``It poses a danger to freedom of expression within the Anglican ministry. </P>
