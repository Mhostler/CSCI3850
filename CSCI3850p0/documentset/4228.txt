<TITLE> Earthquake shakes northern California coast</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Earthquake shakes northern California coast</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-CALIFORNIA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SAN FRANCISCO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A small earthquake shook a coastal area of northern California early Wednesday, but there were no reports of damage or injury, the U.S. Geological Survey said. </P>
<P> The quake, which measured 4.0 on the Richter scale, struck at 1.37 a.m. PST (4.37 a.m. EST) near Eureka, a town on the Pacific coast 220 miles north of San Francisco, said Willis Jacobs, a geophysicist at the Geological Survey's National Earthquake Information Center in Golden, Colo. </P>
<P> He said the quake was felt in the Eureka area but there were no reports of damage or injury. </P>
<P> Earthquake activity is frequent in the area which lies close to the Mendocino Fault, he said. </P>
