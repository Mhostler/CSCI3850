<TITLE> California Gov. seeks federal disaster declaration</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> California Gov. seeks federal disaster declaration</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-WEATHER-WILSON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>FORESTVILLE, Calif.(Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  California Gov. Pete Wilson declared a state of emergency in 11 flood-hit counties Tuesday and asked President Clinton to declare the affected counties federal disaster areas. </P>
<P> During a visit to the flooded wine country of northern California, Wilson announced he was adding 11 counties in both northern and southern California to the list of six counties where he had previously declared states of emergency because of winter storm damage and floods. </P>
<P> He said he had formally asked Clinton to declare all 17 flood-hit counties federal disaster areas, which would make them eligible for federal disaster relief. </P>
<P> California has been hit by torrential rains in the last week which have caused severe flooding. </P>
