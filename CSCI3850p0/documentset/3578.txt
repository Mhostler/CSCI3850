<TITLE> Two people dead, dozens injured in Italian storms</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Two people dead, dozens injured in Italian storms</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-ITALY-WEATHER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ROME (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two people died Wednesday and dozens of people were injured as snow storms and fierce winds swept Italy, blocking roads and railway lines and cutting electricity, civil protection officials said. </P>
<P> An 11-year-old boy was killed and seven of his friends were injured when the roof of a building covered with 10 feet of snow collapsed near the central town of Chieti. </P>
<P> The children, who were all boy scouts taking part in an outdoor trip, had sought refuge from the storms in the building. </P>
<P> A 55-year-old man died of frostbite in his home in the northwestern port city of Genoa, officials said. </P>
<P> Dozens of motorists were injured in road accidents. </P>
<P> Storms in central and southern Italy raging for the past 48 hours left some towns completely isolated. </P>
<P> In northern Italy, temperatures reached record lows. Officials in Marmolada, a town in the northeastern Dolomite mountains, said the temperature had reached minus 13 degrees fahrenheit. </P>
<P> Winds blowing at 50 mph were reported in the northeastern city of Trieste. </P>
<P> In Portugal, two fishermen were killed and one was missing after a large wave sank their outboard motorboat near the northern fishing port of Viana de Castelo Wednesday, firemen said. </P>
