<TITLE> Russian soldiers killed by blast in Chechnya</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Russian soldiers killed by blast in Chechnya</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHECHNYA-BLAST </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  At least 25 Russian servicemen died Saturday in an accidental explosion near the Chechen capital Grozny, where Moscow's troops are battling separatist forces, Itar-Tass news agency reported. </P>
<P> Tass, quoting officials at Russian military headquarters in the town of Mozdok, said the men were killed when an unidentified explosive device blew up early in the morning. </P>
<P> Tass said the victims included members of an OMON special-purpose Interior Ministry unit, regular Interior Ministry troops and staff of the federal counter-intelligence service. </P>
<P> An investigation had already started into the incident. The agency gave no further details. </P>
<P> Russian officials said Friday that 1,146 servicemen had died so far in the conflict, which started Dec. 11 when the Kremlin sent in troops and armor to crush Chechnya's three-year independence bid. </P>
