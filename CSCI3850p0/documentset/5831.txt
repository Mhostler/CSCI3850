<TITLE> Texas gunman charged with attempted murder</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Texas gunman charged with attempted murder</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SHOOTING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>DALLAS, (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A Texas man has been arrested and charged with attempted murder following the shooting of a former co-worker and the kidnapping of another at an east Texas company, police said Tuesday. </P>
<P> Robert Bonner, 31, was arraigned Tuesday on an attempted murder charge and could also face an aggravated kidnapping charge, said Carthage Police Chief Duane Baushke. </P>
<P> Bonner held police at bay in a nearly 12-hour standoff that began Monday morning when he allegedly shot Ernest McNeill, 56, the personnel manager of the Carthage Cup Company, where Bonner had previously worked. McNeill is hospitalized in stable condition. </P>
<P> The suspect then took the company's manufacturing director Steve Indrelunas, 37, hostage, but released him unharmed Monday night. Bonner finally surrendered around 10:30 p.m. </P>
<P> Carthage, about 130 miles east of Dallas, has a population of 6,800. The cup company employs about 150 people. </P>
