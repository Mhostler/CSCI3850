<TITLE> Israeli, troops arrest 31, wound two in West Bank</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Israeli, troops arrest 31, wound two in West Bank</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MIDEAST-UNREST 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES> (Adds army account of Nablus clashes)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>HEBRON, West Bank (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Israeli troops arrested 26 Arabs and five Israeli peace activists during clashes Friday in the occupied West Bank towns of Nablus and Hebron in which two Arabs were wounded, Palestinians and the army said. </P>
<P> Palestinians said troops shot and wounded two protestors in clashes with stone-throwing Palestinians in Nablus and Israeli undercover forces in civilian clothes arrested 25 Palestinians during the fighting. </P>
<P> The army said no Palestinians were shot and only six people were arrested in Nablus. It said one stone-thrower was slightly wounded when he stumbled while soldiers were chasing him. </P>
<P> In Hebron, where a Jewish settler shot dead 29 Muslims at prayer on February 25, 1994, troops arrested five Israeli peace activists during a demonstration, the army said. It said it also arrested one Arab but gave no details. </P>
<P> Palestinians said troops prevented a group of about 30 Israeli peace activists from reaching the Tomb of the Patriarchs complex, where last year's shooting took place. They said scuffles ensued followed by arrests. </P>
<P> The demonstrators carried banners calling for establishment of a Palestinian state and evacuation of Jewish enclaves inside Hebron where about 400 settlers live amid 100,000 Palestinians. </P>
<P> Palestinians said about 3,000 Muslims prayed Friday at the Tomb of the Patriarchs complex, a site is sacred to both Muslims and Jews. </P>
<P> It has been divided into Jewish and Muslim sides but troops barred Jews from the entire site for the last Friday prayers of the Muslim holy month of Ramadan. </P>
