<TITLE> SECURITY GUARD SAYS HE WAS BEATEN BY DRUG RUNNERS</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> SECURITY GUARD SAYS HE WAS BEATEN BY DRUG RUNNERS</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-DRUGS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A man was arrested and charged with attempted murder Wednesday after allegedly attacking an airport security guard in an attempt to get him to clear packages containing drugs through customs. </P>
<P> The man, Johaan Williams, 26, allegedly assaulted the off-duty guard at his home Tuesday night. </P>
<P> A police spokeswoman said that Williams and another man tortured the guard, forced him to drink bleach, placed a hot iron on his left hand and pistol-whipped him. </P>
<P> The guard, Yaw Byass, 23, works for Aviation Safeguard, a security company hired by AMR Services, the ground services wing of AMR Corp, which owns American Airlines. </P>
<P> The spokeswoman said that the men wanted three shipments a week cleared through New York's John F Kennedy International Airport. The origination of the shipments was not known. </P>
<P> A spokesman for AMR said the company was cooperating with authorities in the case. </P>
<P> Williams was also charged with robbery, unlawful imprisonment, burglarly and unlawful possession of a weapon. </P>
