<TITLE> Free U.S.pilot expresses sympathy for dead comrade</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Free U.S.pilot expresses sympathy for dead comrade</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-HELICOPTER-PILOT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>OSAN AIR FORCE BASE, South Korea (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Bobby Hall, the U.S. Army helicopter pilot freed by North Korea Friday, expressed sympathy for his fellow crewman killed when their helicopter crashed. </P>
<P> ``I would like to express my deep sympathy for David Hilemon and his famliy and friends,'' Hall told reporters just before leaving South Korea on a military plane for the United States to be reunited with his family. </P>
<P> ``And I want to say thanks to all the soldiers and Marines and airmen ... for all their help to get me back to the United States and the free world,'' he said. </P>
<P> It was Hall's first public statement since he was freed Friday morning by North Korea where he had been held since his helicopter came down there on Dec. 17. </P>
