<TITLE> Chechen ultimatum stands--Russian Security Council</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Chechen ultimatum stands--Russian Security Council</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHECHNYA-DEADLINE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Russia's powerful Security Council, which met Saturday to discuss the situation in rebel Chechnya, has advised civilians to leave the capital Grozny and reiterated a midnight deadline for Chechens to lay down arms. </P>
<P> A government press office statement carried by Itar-Tass news agency also said the Security Council wanted ``decisive measures'' to be taken to avert armed attacks on Russian soldiers and civilians. </P>
<P> The government press center also said the Security Council had taken decisions aimed at ``speeding up the process of ensuring constitutional order, legality and citizens' rights in Chechnya.'' </P>
<P> ``It is stressed in the decision of the Security Council that from midnight the deadline for an amnesty and the voluntary handover of arms expires. The civilian population is advised to leave the locations of illegal armed formations to avoid casualties,'' the press center statement said. </P>
<P> But there was still a possibility of a peaceful resolution, it said. </P>
<P> President Boris Yeltsin had authorized Deputy Prime Minister Nikolai Yegorov and counter-intelligence chief Sergei Stepashin to meet Dudayev in the North Ossetian town of Mozdok to work out how arms and military equipment could be handed over. </P>
