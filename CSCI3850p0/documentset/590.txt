<TITLE> TYPHOON CAITLIN KILLS EIGHT,FOUR MISSING IN TAIWAN</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> TYPHOON CAITLIN KILLS EIGHT,FOUR MISSING IN TAIWAN</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-TAIWAN-TYPHOON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TAIPEI, Taiwan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Typhoon Caitlin killed at least eight people when it ripped through eastern and central Taiwan with high winds and heavy rain overnight, police said Thursday. </P>
<P> Three people died in the south, one of them electrocuted, and five were killed in a car crash in storms in eastern Hualien County, a police spokesman said. </P>
<P> Four people were reported missing, two of them swept away after going fishing at the southern port of Kaohsiung. </P>
<P> The typhoon cut communications with several districts and major roads connecting the east and west coasts were blocked by flooding and landslides. </P>
<P> Airports in the south and east and on offshore islands were forced to close. The storm cut power to more than 140,000 households for several hours. </P>
<P> Estimates of damage were not immediately available. </P>
<P> Typhoon Caitlin was heading toward the Chinese coast with Typhoon Doug now approaching Taiwan, the Central Weather Bureau said. </P>
<P> Typhoon Tim battered the island last month, killing 17 people. </P>
