<TITLE> Two Americans awarded $510,000 Japan prize</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Two Americans awarded $510,000 Japan prize</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-JAPAN-PRIZE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two American scientists have been awarded a $510,000 prize each from the Science and Technology Foundation of Japan for their work in semiconductors and the environment, the foundation said Wednesday. </P>
<P> Nick Holonyak, 66, of the University of Illinois, and Edward Knipling, 85, professor emiritus at Florida State University, were awarded the prize, the Japan-based foundation said in a statement released here. </P>
<P> Holonyak, of Urbana, Ill., earned the Japan Prize in materials processing technology for his work on the physical principles of certain semiconductors, known as intermetallic compound semiconductors. </P>
<P> Knipling, of Arlington, Va., was cited for his work on biological approaches to pest management strategies such as the release of sterile insects. </P>
