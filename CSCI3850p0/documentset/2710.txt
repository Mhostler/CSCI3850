<TITLE> Leisure Air suspends flights after review, FAA says</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Leisure Air suspends flights after review, FAA says</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FAA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Leisure Air, which operates scheduled and charter flights, suspended all flights following a weeklong safety inspection, the Federal Aviation Administration said. </P>
<P> The FAA said Leisure Air failed to demonstrate its ability to comply fully with its airline safety standards. </P>
<P> The airline, based in Winston-Salem, N.C., operated scheduled flights between Orlando and West Palm Beach, Fla., Atlanta, Los Angeles, Boston, and Hartford, Conn. It also provided charter service to Mexico, the Caribbean and Hawaii. </P>
<P> In a statement, the FAA said it conducted a weeklong special safety inspection of Leisure Air. The inspection focused on compliance with safety rules governing flight operations and aircraft maintenance. </P>
<P> The airline will not be permitted to resume flights until the FAA is satisfied Leisure Air complies with safety standards. </P>
<P> Leisure Air has eight aircraft in its fleet, including five Airbus 320s, a Boeing 757 and two McDonnell-Douglas DC-10s, the FAA said. </P>
