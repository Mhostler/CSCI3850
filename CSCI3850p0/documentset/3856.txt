<TITLE> Hong Kong tourist arrested in Canada heroin seizure</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Hong Kong tourist arrested in Canada heroin seizure</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-DRUGS-CANADA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>VANCOUVER (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Canadian customs inspectors at Vancouver's airport seized heroin worth an estimated $5 million that was found taped to the body of a tourist from Hong Kong, officials said Thursday. </P>
<P> Hong Kong resident Lai Heung Hung, 39, was arrested after bricks of the drug weighing 6.6 pounds were found taped to her waist and thighs. </P>
<P> She was part of a tour group arriving for a vacation in Canada and inspectors discovered the drugs during a routine inspection, officials said. </P>
<P> Police also arrested U.S. resident Shing Yam and Xie Long, an immigrant to Canada, who they said were due to receive the heroin. </P>
