<TITLE> Pope beatifies five at Vatican ceremony</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pope beatifies five at Vatican ceremony</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-POPE-BEATIFICATION (PICTURE) </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>VATICAN CITY (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Pope John Paul Sunday beatified a Venezuelan, a German and three Italians who founded religious orders in the 19th and 20th centuries. </P>
<P> The 74-year-old Pontiff beatified the four nuns and a priest at a solemn ceremony attend by tens of thousands of people in St Peter's Square. </P>
<P> Maria de San Jose Alvarado Cardozo, who founded an order of nuns to help the elderly and orphans in the early 1900s, became Venezuela's first candidate for sainthood. </P>
<P> Beatification is the penultimate step leading to sainthood in the Roman Catholic Church. </P>
<P> Venezuelan President Rafael Caldera headed his country's delegation to the beatification ceremonies. </P>
<P></P>
<P> - Agostino Roscelli, an Italian priest who lived from 1818 to 1902 and founded the order of the Sisters of the Immaculate to teach and work in hospitals. </P>
<P> - Helena Stollenwerk, a German who lived from 1852 to 1900 and co-founded the Mission Congregation of the Holy Spirit. Its missionaries worked in China, Africa and South America. </P>
<P> - Maria Domenica Brun Barbantini, an Italian who lived from 1789 to 1868 who founded the Sisters of St Camillo to help the sick and dying. </P>
<P> - Giuseppina Gabriella Bonino, an Italian who lived from 1843 to 1906 and founded the Sisters of the Holy Family to provide education for orphans. </P>
