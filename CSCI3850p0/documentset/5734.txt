<TITLE> Indonesian troops wanted in Africa, agency says</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Indonesian troops wanted in Africa, agency says</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-INDONESIA-PEACEKEEPING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>JAKARTA, April 14 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Indonesia's armed forces chief has said the United Nations asked Jakarta to contribute troops to U.N. peacekeeping forces in African countries, the official Antara news agency said on Friday. </P>
<P> It quoted General Feisal Tanjung as saying in New York on Wednesday that the request was formally made by U.N. Secretary-General Boutros Boutros-Ghali during a meeting to discuss Indonesia's roles in U.N.'s peacekeeping missions. </P>
<P> ``When we touched on Indonesia's participation in the U.N. peacekeeping force in the former Yugoslavia, the secretary-general said the performance of the Indonesian contingent was outstanding,'' he said. </P>
<P> Indonesia sent a health-care battalion to the former Yugoslavia. </P>
<P> The general said Boutros-Ghali asked Indonesia to be ready for deployment in African countries such as Angola, Burundi or Rwanda. </P>
<P> Tanjung, speaking at a dinner hosted by Indonesia's permanent representive to the United Nations, Nugroho Wisnumurti, said a formal reply to the secretary-general's request would be made after a consultation with Indonesia's Foreign Ministry. </P>
<P> ``But the Indonesian armed forces are ready at any time to take part in peace mission,'' he said. </P>
<P> Indonesia has planned to send a sapper's battalion to the war-torn former Yugoslavia to help in minesweeping and programmes to rehabilitate residential areas. It will also replace the 236-strong Indonesian medical team currently in Bosnia. </P>
<P> Tanjung also said during the meeting with Boutros-Ghali that Indonesia had submitted a proposal that it be given a chance to head a U.N. peacekeeping force somewhere in the world. </P>
