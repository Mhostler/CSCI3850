<TITLE> U.S., S. Korea, Japan leaders to meet on N.Korea</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.S., S. Korea, Japan leaders to meet on N.Korea</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-APEC-KOREA-MEETING 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>(Eds: Recasts with Clinton confirmation)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>JAKARTA, Indonesia (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The leaders of the United States, Japan and South Korea, eager to boost a landmark nuclear accord with North Korea, were to meet in Jakarta Monday, President Clinton said. </P>
<P> Clinton told a news conference he would hold a short meeting with South Korean President Kim Young-sam and Japanese Prime Minister Tomiichi Murayama and stressed the importance of the nuclear deal for the security and stability of Asia and the rest of the world. </P>
<P> The United States and North Korea signed the accord -- which commits Pyongyang to freeze and then dismantle its nuclear program in return for over $4.0 billion in internationally financed nuclear technology and interim energy supplies -- in Geneva on Oct. 21. </P>
<P> The three men are among leaders representing half the world's population gathered in Indonesia for the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) summit. </P>
<P> In Pyongyang Monday, nuclear experts from the United States and North Korea opened talks on the safe storage of spent nuclear fuel in a dialog that took place under the terms of the Oct. 21 agreement. </P>
<P> The specific focus of the negotiations in Pyongyang is to ensure the safe storage of 8,000 spent nuclear fuel rods removed last summer from an experimental graphite reactor. </P>
