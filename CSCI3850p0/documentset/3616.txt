<TITLE> Carter appoints South African rights expert</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Carter appoints South African rights expert</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SAFRICA-CARTER </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ATLANTA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  South African human rights activist Johan David van der Vyver has joined former President Jimmy Carter's staff of international rights experts, the Carter Center announced Friday. </P>
<P> While serving as a Carter Center fellow, Van der Vyver will work on Carter Center projects aimed at preventing human rights abuses in emerging democracies in Africa and Latin America. </P>
<P> He also will become a law professor at Emory University, with which the Carter Center is affiliated. </P>
<P> ``Dr van der Vyver joins us at a key time for the Center's human rights program, which in December began to work with a new International Human Rights Council to renew and advance the cause of human rights worldwide,'' Carter said in a statement. </P>
<P> The council, chaired by Carter, consists of human rights activists from around the world including the Nigerian Nobel laureate, Wole Soyinka. </P>
<P> The appointee was described as a widely renowned authority on international human rights and comparative constitutional law whose contributions to the fight against apartheid helped to establish democracy in South Africa. </P>
