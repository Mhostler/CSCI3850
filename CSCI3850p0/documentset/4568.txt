<TITLE> Egyptian army finds World War II truck in desert</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Egyptian army finds World War II truck in desert</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-TRUCK </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAIRO, Egypt (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Egyptian army has recovered a World War II supply truck lost in the Western Desert more than 50 years ago, Al-Hayat newspaper reported Sunday. </P>
<P> The vehicle, which had belonged to the British-led Allies, was more than 1,300 miles from the town of Al-Alamein, where the Allies fought a major tank battle with German forces in 1942. </P>
<P> The truck was loaded with gas containers, milk produced in 1941, bullets, oil and other supplies. It was spotted by an Egyptian military aircraft on a routine patrol, officials told the newspaper. </P>
<P> Egyptian army mechanics said the vehicle was in good condition and they had been able to fix its engine and tires. </P>
