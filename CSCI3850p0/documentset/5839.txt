<TITLE> Baseball-Jack Morris retires</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Baseball-Jack Morris retires</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BASEBALL-MORRIS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>PLANT CITY, Fla.(Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Jack Morris, baseball's most productive pitcher of the 1980s and a member of four World Series-winning teams, has decided to retire, the Cincinnati Reds announced Tuesday. </P>
<P> Morris, who turns 40 in a month, had more wins, innings pitched and complete games than anyone else in the 1980s, and retires with the most victories of any active pitcher. </P>
<P> The right-hander signed with the Reds as a free agent this season after spending the first 16 years of his career in the American League. Morris was 10-6 with a 5.60 earned run average with the Cleveland Indians last season. </P>
<P> Morris's World Series-winning teams included the Detroit Tigers in 1984, the Minnesota Twins in 1991 and the Toronto Blues Jays in 1992 and 1993. In seven World Series starts, Morris was 4-2 with a 2.96 ERA. He did not pitch for Toronto in the 1993 World Series. </P>
<P> Morris pitched all 10 innings of Minnesota's 1-0 shutout of the Atlanta Braves in Game Seven of the 1991 World Series. </P>
<P> Morris had a no-hitter on April 7, 1984, for the Tigers against the Chicago White Sox. </P>
<P> Morris was 254-186 (.577) with a 3.90 career ERA in 549 games and 3,824-1/3 innings. </P>
