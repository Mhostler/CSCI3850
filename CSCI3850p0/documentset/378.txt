<TITLE> CHINA'S GUANGDONG BANS POLICE FROM SEX TRADE</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> CHINA'S GUANGDONG BANS POLICE FROM SEX TRADE</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-POLICE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>HONG KONG (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Police in China's Guangdong province have been banned from owning or managing massage parlors, or prostitution and narcotics-related businesses, the Beijing-controlled Wen Wei Po newspaper said Monday. </P>
<P> A recent directive issued by the public security bureau banned direct or indirect interests in the sex and drug trades, the paper said. Those with such interests must quit by the end of July. </P>
<P> Public security officials are also banned from participating in opening ceremonies for sex and drug businesses, it said. </P>
<P> China's police and military in recent years have increasingly indulged in money-making activities ranging from exporting missiles to running hostess clubs. </P>
