<TITLE> THOUSANDS FLEE CORSICA FIRES, ARSON SUSPECTS HELD</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> THOUSANDS FLEE CORSICA FIRES, ARSON SUSPECTS HELD</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FRANCE-CORSICA-FIRES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>AJACCIO, Corsica (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Thousands of tourists and residents fled as forest fires blazed through southern Corsica Friday, and authorities questioned three arson suspects. </P>
<P> Fanned by 50-mph winds, the fires threaten towns and villages and have already killed a woman and injured a soldier. </P>
<P> A thousand firefighters, backed by a dozen planes and helicopters dumping tankloads of water, struggled to control flames which have destroyed more than 12,500 acres of forest and brush this week. </P>
<P> Three people suspected of setting the fires were detained for questioning. </P>
<P> Five thousand tourists and residents who fled in the path of flames gathered at a sport stadium in Porto Vecchio before being dispatched to schools and gymnasiums for the night. </P>
<P> Two hundred-sixty soldiers and emergency workers arrived on the Mediterranean island in the afternoon with bulldozers, and authorities asked the Interior Ministry for more reinforcements. </P>
