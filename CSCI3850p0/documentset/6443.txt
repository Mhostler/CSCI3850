<TITLE> State Department's Lord sets Asia tour</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> State Department's Lord sets Asia tour</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-VIETNAM-USA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The State Department's top East Asia policymaker will visit Vietnam and Laos next week as part of a seven-stop regional tour, the department said Wednesday. </P>
<P> Winston Lord, assistant secretary of state for East Asia and the Pacific, will be in Asia on May 13-26 to discuss ``regional, bilateral and global issues with a wide range of public and private sector leaders,'' the department said. </P>
<P> In Vietnam, along with Deputy Secretary for Veterans Affairs Hershel Gober, Lord is to head a special presidential mission aimed at assessing Hanoi's cooperation on efforts to account for the 2,205 Americans still listed as missing in action from the Indochina wars, which ended 20 years ago last month. </P>
<P> The White House is expected to make its own announcement designating the Vietnam leg a presidential mission, State Department officials said. </P>
<P> Lord will visit Japan on May 18 and 19 for consultations on a broad range of issues with Japanese officials, before continuing to Hong Kong. </P>
<P> He then will visit Brunei to attend a senior officials meeting for the ASEAN regional forum, followed by a stop in Singapore on May 24-25 to meet host government leaders, the department said. </P>
