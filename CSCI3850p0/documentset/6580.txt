<TITLE> Chemistry Nobel prize winner dead at 78</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Chemistry Nobel prize winner dead at 78</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NOBEL-ANFINSEN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Christian Anfinsen, who won the 1972 Nobel Prize for chemistry, died Sunday at a hospital in Randallstown, Md., after a heart attack, the Washington Post reported Tuesday. He was 78. </P>
<P> Anfinsen, a former National Institutes of Health laboratory director and Johns Hopkins University professor, won half the Nobel Prize for research he began in the 1950s on enzyme ribonuclease that determined the primary structure of proteins. </P>
<P> He shared the award with Rockefeller University scientists Stanford Moore and William Stein. </P>
<P> After winning the Nobel Prize, Anfinsen became interested in interferon, the protein that helps the body defend itself against infection and cancer. </P>
<P> He also began working for human rights causes, including to free scientists imprisoned in Latin America. </P>
<P> Since 1982, he had been a professor at Johns Hopkins University, focusing most recently on the study of bacteria discovered along the edges of tectonic plates in the Mediterrean Sea and Pacific Ocean floors. </P>
<P> He was exploring the use of those bacteria, which can survive high temperatures, to dispose of toxic wastes and chemical weapons. </P>
