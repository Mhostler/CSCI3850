<TITLE> AFTERSHOCK OF 1992 QUAKE HITS CALIFORNIA DESERT</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> AFTERSHOCK OF 1992 QUAKE HITS CALIFORNIA DESERT</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-CALIFORNIA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LOS ANGELES (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A strong aftershock of the 1992 Landers earthquake rumbled through desert communities east of Los Angeles Sunday but there were no reports of damage or injuries. </P>
<P> The aftershock, measuring 4.0 on the Richter scale, hit at 8:10 a.m. PDT (11:10 a.m. EDT, and was centered 13 miles southeast of Yucca Valley, a small town about 100 miles from Los Angeles, the California Institute of Technology seismology lab reported. </P>
<P> It was one of thousands of quakes that have followed the 7.5-magnitude Landers and 6.6-magnitude Big Bear quakes, twin shakers that struck the desert area June 28, 1992, within hours of each other. </P>
<P> The Landers quake was California's strongest in 40 years and killed a three-year-old boy. Together the two quakes injured more than 400 people and left more than $90 million in damage. </P>
