<TITLE> NHL-Blackhawks reacquire Denis Savard from Tampa Bay</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> NHL-Blackhawks reacquire Denis Savard from Tampa Bay</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NHL-SAVARD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CHICAGO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Veteran center Denis Savard is back with the Chicago Blackhawks, who acquired him from the Tampa Bay Lightning for a sixth-round pick in the 1996 draft Thursday. </P>
<P> Savard, 34, played 10 seasons with the Blackhawks from 1980 to 1990 before he was traded to Montreal in exchange for Chris Chelios and a draft choice in June of 1990. </P>
<P> Savard played three seasons with the Canadiens and was a member of the 1993 Stanley Cup-winning team. </P>
<P> He signed as a free agent with Tampa Bay during the summer of 1993 and has played the last two seasons with the Lightning. Savard had six goals and 11 assists for 17 points in 31 games this season for Tampa Bay. </P>
<P> Savard was originally a first-round pick and the third overall selection in the 1980 draft. </P>
<P> Savard has played in 1,051 career NHL games and was the 96th player in league history to play in 1,000 career games. </P>
<P> Savard has 447 goals and 808 assists for 1,255 points during his 15-year NHL career. He recorded his 1,200th career point on Oct. 27, 1993, becoming only the 12th player in NHL history to reach that milestone. </P>
<P> Savard has played 736 regular-season games in a Blackhawk uniform and ranks near the top of all the club all-time offensive categories. He is fourth on the team's all-time goal scoring list (351), second on the all-time assist list (662) and third on the all-time point list (1,013). </P>
