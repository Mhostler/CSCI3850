<TITLE> Fiftieth Karachi policemen killed since December</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Fiftieth Karachi policemen killed since December</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PAKISTAN-VIOLENCE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KARACHI, Pakistan (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Gunmen ambushed and shot dead a police inspector in Karachi Thursday, bringing to 50 the number of policemen slain since the army pulled out of the bloodstained southern Pakistani city Nov. 30. </P>
<P> A police spokesman said Inspector Hakim Khan Tanooli was killed by gunmen on motorcycles as he left for work in a police vehicle. Tanooli's driver and a bodyguard were unhurt. </P>
<P> At least 10 policemen have been killed in the last two weeks alone, including two Wednesday, one of whom had been kidnapped a day earlier, the spokesman said. </P>
<P> At least one soldier and seven paramilitary rangers have been killed and several other security personnel wounded since troops quit Karachi after a 29 months of fighting ethnic and sectarian violence. </P>
<P> At least 400 people, including members of the security forces, have died in Karachi this year. About 800 were killed in the city last year. </P>
