<TITLE> LAWYERS DELAY TRIAL IN ``HOUSE OF HORROR'' CASE</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> LAWYERS DELAY TRIAL IN ``HOUSE OF HORROR'' CASE</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BRITAIN-BODIES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>GLOUCESTER, England (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A British couple accused of murdering nine young women were ordered kept in custody on Thursday while lawyers prepare for trial. </P>
<P> Frederick and Rosemary West have been held for several months, since police started digging up bodies in the so-called ``House of Horror'' where the couple lived, another former residence and two fields. </P>
<P> West, 52, a builder, and Rosemary, his second wife, aged 40, were remanded in custody for another month after making a brief appearance at Gloucester Magistrates Court. </P>
<P> West is accused of another three murders, those of his first wife Catherine, their eight-year-old daughter, Charmaine and family nanny, Ann McFall. </P>
<P> Tony Miles, who recently took over as West's defense lawyer, asked the court for further time to consider the prosecution documents. </P>
