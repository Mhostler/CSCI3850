<TITLE> Singapore hangs two Thais, Malaysian for drugs</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Singapore hangs two Thais, Malaysian for drugs</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-DRUGS-SINGAPORE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SINGAPORE (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Two Thai men and a Malaysian convicted in Singapore of drug trafficking were hanged Friday, bringing the number of people executed under the city-state's tough drug laws to 102. </P>
<P> Don Promphinit, 31, and Krishna Maikham, 32, were executed Friday morning, Singapore's Central Narcotics Bureau said. The men, construction workers, were both Thai nationals who were arrested for trafficking on Jan. 10, 1993. </P>
<P> Nasrul Esyam bin Shamsudin, a 21-year-old Malaysian landscape artist, was also executed Friday morning. He was arrested Sept. 30, 1993, after he was stopped at the border checkpoint from Malaysia to Singapore. </P>
<P> Appeals by all three men were dimissed. </P>
<P> Under Singapore's drug laws, the death sentence is mandatory for anyone over 18 years of age found guilty of trafficking in more than 15 grams (half an ounce) of heroin, 30 grams (an ounce) of morphine or 500 grams (18 ounces) of cannabis. </P>
<P> Filipina maid Flor Contemplacion, convicted of a double murder, was also hanged Friday at Changi Prison. </P>
