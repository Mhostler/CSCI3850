<TITLE> Eight-week trail in Japan gas attack</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Eight-week trail in Japan gas attack</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-JAPAN-SUBWAY-CHRONOLOGY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TOKYO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The formal naming Monday of doomsday cult leader Shoko Asahara as responsible for the Tokyo subway gas attack comes eight weeks to the day after the deadly sarin nerve gas poured through the system. </P>
<P></P>
<P> March 20 - Sarin is released on five trains on three lines in Tokyo's subway sytem, killing 12 people and injuring 5,000. </P>
<P> March 22 - Police begin daily raids on buildings used by Aum Shinri Kyo (Supreme Truth Sect), a doomsday cult believed involved in the attack since a sarin gas leak was reported at its headquarters near Mount Fuji two years earlier. </P>
<P> March 25 - Asahara, the sect's elusive leader, says in video message that U.S. troops sprayed him and his followers with nerve gas. ``I am seriously sick,'' Ashara says, ``some 50 percent of my 1,700 pupils are troubled with sickness ... The gas was sprayed by U.S. troops, unmistakably.'' </P>
<P> March 30 - A masked gunman seriously wounds national police chief Takaji Kunimatsu. Anonymous calls to television networks link the shooting to the subway attacks. </P>
<P> April 13 - In a television interview, an unidentified sect member warns of an impending disaster on a scale far greater than the Kobe earthquake, which killed 5,500 people. Senior cult official Kiyohide Nakada arrested on extortion charges. </P>
<P> April 19 - Nearly 400 people are rushed to hospitals in Yokohama, Japan's second largest city, after a World War I style gas is released into railroad system there. Hours later police arrest Kiyohide Hayakawa, a sect leader, on charges involving a break-in at a garage. </P>
<P> April 21 - A poison-gas attack sends Japan into panic for the third time; 25 people are overcome by fumes in a store 300 yards from the Yokohama station attacked two days earlier. </P>
<P> April 22 - Using physical evidence, including homemade plastic bags and containers, police reportedly link the cult to the original Tokyo gas attack. </P>
<P> April 23 - Hideo Murai, the second-highest ranking official of the Aum Shinri Kyo sect, is stabbed outside its headquarters in Tokyo. Murai dies seven hours later. </P>
<P> April 27 - The national police agency orders a nationwide search for sect leader Asahara. </P>
<P> May 6 - Guards thwart a poison-gas attack which could have killed 20,000 in Tokyo's largest subway station, defusing a device in a bathroom before it can release deadly cyanide gas. </P>
<P> May 11 - Police arrest Kenji Kamimine from Japan's largest underworld group as an accomplice in the Murai murder. </P>
<P> May 13 - A bomb explodes at Tokyo international airport and 30 mystery balloons float to earth over Japan, sparking new security concerns. Kyodo news agency says hard-line cult members may resort to terrortism if their leader is arrested. </P>
<P> May 15 - Police capture the suspected mastermind of the Tokyo attack and murder warrants are issued against Asahara and 40 followers in the first formal linking of the sect to the attack. </P>
<P> In new security scare mystery fumes engulf a major subway station in Yokohama, overcoming 20 people including three who are rushed to a hospital. There is no immediate indication the fumes are linked to previous attacks. </P>
