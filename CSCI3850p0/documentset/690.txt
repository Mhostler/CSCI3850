<TITLE> MINOR DERAILMENT DELAYS NIGHT OWL TO BOSTON</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> MINOR DERAILMENT DELAYS NIGHT OWL TO BOSTON</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-AMTRAK </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The last car of the Night Owl, an overnight Amtrak train from Washington to Boston, derailed at New Haven, Connecticut, delaying the train's arrival in Boston, Amtrak said. </P>
<P> There were no injuries, a spokeswoman said. </P>
<P> According to Amtrak, the derailment involved a mail car. Its rear wheels left the track just east of Amtrak's New Haven station at 5:30 a.m. EDT. At about 7 a.m., the car was separated from the rest of the train, which proceeded to Boston. </P>
<P> Passengers ``were delayed for about 90 minutes,'' the spokeswoman said. </P>
<P> The number of passengers on the train was not immediately available. </P>
