<TITLE> Pennsylvania firm recalls processed beef</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pennsylvania firm recalls processed beef</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FOOD-RECALL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Food processing firm C.D. Moyer Co. of Silverdale, Penn., has recalled 1,000 lbs of beef franks because of possible contamination the listeria bacteria, the Agriculture Department said Wednesday. </P>
<P> Franks under the brand names Kohler and Cefolo's Pride with a ``sell by'' date of Feb. 22 could be contaminated by the listeria bacteria, which was discovered in routine inspection, USDA said in a statement. </P>
<P> ``We urge consumers who have purchased the suspect product to return it to the place of purchase,'' USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service Administrator Michael Taylor said. </P>
<P> USDA also said Morehead Food Products of Amarillo, Tx., late last week recalled 5,000 lbs of processed foods after an employee was diagnosed with the hepatitus A virus. </P>
