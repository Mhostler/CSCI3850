<TITLE> Quayle hospitalized for appendicitis</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Quayle hospitalized for appendicitis</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAYLE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>INDIANAPOLIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Former vice president Dan Quayle has been admitted to a hospital suffering from appendicitis and will probably undergo surgery this week, his spokeswoman said Tuesday. </P>
<P> Quayle, 47, entered Indiana University Medical Center Monday afternoon, spokeswoman Ann Hathaway said, adding that the course of treatment will probably take seven days. </P>
<P> Quayle, who is expected to announce his bid for the 1996 Republican presidential nomination soon, was discharged from the same hospital Dec. 6 after treatment for blood clots in his lungs. Hathaway said he was suffering from an enlarged appendix and would probably undergo surgery this week. </P>
