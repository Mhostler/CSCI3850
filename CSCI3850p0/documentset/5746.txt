<TITLE> Suspected Muslim militants kill six in Egypt</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Suspected Muslim militants kill six in Egypt</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-MILITANTS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAIRO, Egypt (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Suspected Muslim militants shot and killed six people, including a three-year-old girl and a policeman, in a village in southern Egypt Friday, police said. </P>
<P> Unidentified gunmen opened fire in a busy street in the village of Nawai in Minya province, 160 miles south of Cairo, killing three-year-old Asmaa Hassan el-Sayed, two women, two civilian men and policeman Saber Mahmoud Abdel-Hakim, they said. </P>
<P> They said they believed the attackers were members of Egypt's largest militant organization, al-Gamaa al-Islamiya (Islamic Group). </P>
<P> The Gamaa has been leading a campaign of violence to overthrew President Hosni Mubarak's government and establish a strict Islamic state since 1992. More than 710 people, including civilians, policemen and militants, have been killed in the campaign. </P>
<P> Minya province has been at the center of clashes between police and militants this year. Militants use its vast sugarcane fields and mountains as hideouts to carry out hit-and-run attacks against policemen. </P>
