<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-TALKS-STATEMENT -2 GENEVA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> The joint statement, released by North Korean officials in Geneva, said the two countries were prepared to establish ``diplomatic representation in each other's capitals ... as a move toward full normalisation of political and economic relations.'' </P>
<P> Technical experts will continue talks on issues including replacing North Korea's graphite reactors with light-water reactor power plants. North Korean and U.S. officials agreed to recess high-level talks and resume in Geneva September 23. </P>
<P> Assistant Secretary of State Robert Gallucci headed the American delegation in week-long negotiations with a North Korean delegation led by Deputy Foreign Minister Kang Sok-Ju. </P>
<P> The two sides clinched the accord at two closed-door sessions held late on Friday and in the early hours of Saturday at North Korea's diplomatic mission along Lake Geneva. </P>
