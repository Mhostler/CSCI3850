<TITLE> Frenchman released in Mogadishu - aid officials</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Frenchman released in Mogadishu - aid officials</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SOMALIA-HOSTAGE URGENT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NAIROBI, Kenya (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A French aid worker was released by kidnappers in Mogadishu, Somalia, after five weeks in captivity and was flown to the Kenyan capital Nairobi, aid officials said Sunday. </P>
<P> They said 24-year-old Rudy Marq was released at the U.N. World Food Program (WFP) residence compound in southern Mogadishu Saturday evening and was in good health. </P>
<P> ``Rudy Marq was freed yesterday evening. He is arriving in Nairobi aboard a WFP (World Food Program) plane this morning,'' said WFP spokeswoman Brenda Barton. ``He is in good condition.'' </P>
<P> In the Somali capital Mogadishu, officials of the U.N. Operation in Somalia and WFP confirmed Marq's release, saying it was negotiated by clan elders and no ransom was paid. </P>
<P> Marq, 24, worked for Action Internationale Contre la Faim (AICF-International Action against Hunger), which denied U.N. and aid agency reports of his release Saturday. </P>
