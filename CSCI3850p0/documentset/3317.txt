<P> Surbert told a news conference two of those arrested were citizens of the former Soviet Union, but he refused to say which country they came from. The material, which was safely packed, had carried a certificate. </P>
<P> ``According to the certificate it is highly enriched uranium,'' Subert said. ``Uranium 235 functions as an explosive and it is used in the warhead of rockets.'' </P>
<P> Police caught the three, who were unarmed, as they drove through a suburb of Prague and had known in advance that they would be there, he added. </P>
<P> This was the biggest seizure of such material in the Czech Republic, Subert said. </P>
