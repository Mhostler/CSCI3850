<TITLE> FRANCE, BRITAIN URGE BOSNIA SETTLEMENT</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> FRANCE, BRITAIN URGE BOSNIA SETTLEMENT</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-YUGOSLAVIA-MINISTERS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SARAJEVO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  British and French ministers arrived in Sarajevo Wednesday to press warring Serbs and Muslims to sign the latest international peace plan for Bosnia. </P>
<P> French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe said: ``We hope to convince all the parties that it's time to sign the peace now, to accept the settlement from Geneva.'' </P>
<P></P>
<P> ``The message to everyone is the same. It's a good plan. All those who wish Bosnia well hope the plan will be accepted. I hope everyone understands the consequences if the plan is rejected.'' </P>
<P> The ministers were holding talks with leaders of the Muslim-Croat federation in Sarajevo before going on to the Bosnian Serb stronghold of Pale and then to Belgrade for a meeting with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic. </P>
<P> Hurd and Juppe have said rejection of the plan could open the way to a possible wider war in the Balkans. </P>
