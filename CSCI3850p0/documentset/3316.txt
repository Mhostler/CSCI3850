<TITLE> Carter crosses Sarajevo front line to meet Serbs</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Carter crosses Sarajevo front line to meet Serbs</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-YUGOSLAVIA-CARTER-CROSSING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SARAJEVO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Former President Jimmy Carter Monday crossed over the confrontation line dividing Sarajevo on his way for talks with Bosnian Serb leaders, who have invited him to mediate peace negotiations. </P>
<P> Carter's motorcade drove over the bridge of Brotherhood and Unity -- now a symbol of the city's 32-month siege -- to the Serb-held part of the Bosnian capital at about 9:35 a.m. local (3:35 a.m. EST). </P>
<P> The ex-president took a brief tour of the shattered city by car before heading off for talks with Bosnian Serb chief Radovan Karadzic and other leaders in the mountain village of Pale, which serves as the capital of the Serbs' self-styled state. </P>
<P> Carter had talks with Croatian President Franjo Tudjman and Bosnian President Alija Iztebegovic on the first day of his visit to former Yugoslavia Sunday. </P>
