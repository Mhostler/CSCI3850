<TITLE> Weather keeps shuttle up a little longer</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Weather keeps shuttle up a little longer</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SPACE-SHUTTLE-DELAY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAPE CANAVERAL, Fla (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Persistent clouds forced the Discovery astronauts to pass up another landing opportunity in Florida, NASA said Tuesday. </P>
<P> Mission managers delayed the shuttle's landing by at least 90 minutes. Discovery's next opportunity to touch down would come at 3:44 p.m. EDT at the Kennedy Space Center. </P>
<P> Officials did not say immediately whether the crew would be permitted to land in Florida or diverted to a backup runway in California, where skies were clearer. </P>
