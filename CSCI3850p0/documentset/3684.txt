<P> From the House historian's office, she issued a brief statement that said: ``I seem to have been fired in the press. Let me say that the charges against me are slanderous and outrageous. I have nothing to say at this time.'' </P>
<P> An aide to Gingrich said Monday night that Jeffrey had been fired after the new Republican speaker learned about her comments made in 1986 when she critiqued a Department of Education teaching program on the Holocaust. </P>
<P> She gave an assessment of the program as a volunteer evaluator, Gingrich spokesman Tony Blankley said. </P>
<P> In 1986, she said the Education Department's junior high school program on the Holocaust ``gives no evidence of balance. The Nazi point of view, however unpopular, is still a point of view, and it is not presented,'' she reportedly wrote. ``Nor is that of the Ku Klux Klan.'' </P>
