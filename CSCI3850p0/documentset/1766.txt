<TITLE> Andre Lwoff, Nobel Prize winner, dies in Paris</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Andre Lwoff, Nobel Prize winner, dies in Paris</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-FRANCE-LWOFF </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>PARIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Andre Lwoff, a scientist at the prestigious Pasteur Institute and a winner of the Nobel Prize for Medicine in 1965, died in Paris over the weekend aged 92, the institute said Monday. </P>
<P> The French researcher was a renowned expert in the fields of microbiology, virology and cancer research. </P>
<P> He shared the 1965 Nobel Prize with Francois Jacob and Jacques Monod for their work in cellular biology. </P>
<P> Lwoff held numerous honorary degrees and was a member of scientific societies across Europe, in the United States and in Russia. </P>
