<TITLE> More British troops leave Northern Ireland</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> More British troops leave Northern Ireland</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-IRISH-TROOPS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BELFAST, April 21 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Another 400 British troops were withdrawn from Northern Ireland on Friday in the latest peace dividend since the IRA declared a ceasefire last year. </P>
<P> It was the second major withdrawal of troops from the province. </P>
<P> Up to 1,000 soldiers have now been pulled out since the Irish Republican Army announced its truce last September after waging a 25-year guerrilla campaign to try to end British rule in Northern Ireland. Some 17,500 troops are still in the province. </P>
<P> Soldiers have stopped patrolling the streets of the war-weary province and more withdrawals are expected. </P>
