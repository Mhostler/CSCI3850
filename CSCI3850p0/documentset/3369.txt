<TITLE> IDB approves $100 million loan to Peru</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> IDB approves $100 million loan to Peru</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PERU-BANK </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Inter-American Development Bank said it has approved a $100 million loan to finance the establishment, expansion and improvement of Peru's private sector. </P>
<P> It said the Corporation for Development Finance (Cofide) would execute the loan, rediscounting the funds to intermediary financial institutions, which in turn would pass lend them to private enterprises. </P>
<P> Private companies will be able to use the funds to buy fixed assets, pay for technical and administrative support services and finance the exports of capital goods, consumer durables and engineering services. </P>
<P> The loan is for 10 years and carries a variable interest rate. Local counterpart funds amount to $67 million, to be contributed by Cofide, intermediary financial institutions and private borrowers. </P>
