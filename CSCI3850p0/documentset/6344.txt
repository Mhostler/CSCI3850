<TITLE> Israelis kill two Hizbollah guerrillas in Lebanon</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Israelis kill two Hizbollah guerrillas in Lebanon</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-ISRAEL-LEBANON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MARJAYOUN, Lebanon (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Israeli forces killed two Hizbollah guerrillas Sunday in exchanges of fire in south Lebanon in which two Israeli soldiers were wounded, Hizbollah radio said. </P>
<P> Voice of the Light radio, which broadcasts from Beirut, said two guerrillas of the pro-Iranian Hizbollah (Party of God) were killed in the confrontation with the Israelis that erupted after they attacked a string of Israeli and allied militia posts. </P>
<P> Sources with the Israeli-backed South Lebanon Army militia said two Israeli soldiers were wounded when mortar bombs fired by guerrillas slammed into the fortified Dabsheh outpost on the edge of Israel's south Lebanon occupation zone. </P>
<P> In Jerusalem, an Israeli army spokesman said the two were lightly injured when guerrillas fired missiles and mortars at Israeli positions in the eastern sector of the zone. </P>
<P> The SLA sources said the guerrillas also attacked with anti-tank rockets and machineguns a militia post at Sojoud and fired two Katyusha rockets into the zone town of Marjayoun, where the SLA maintains its headquarters, but no one was hurt. </P>
<P> A Lebanese civilian was wounded near the market town of Nabatiyeh when Israeli and SLA artillery gunners shelled towns and villages, including guerrilla-held ones, north of the zone in retaliation for the attacks, security sources said. </P>
<P> The Muslim militant Hizbollah is waging a guerrilla war on Israel's estimated 1,000 troops in the nine-mile-wide southern Lebanon border zone to force them out. </P>
