<TITLE> Hill becomes first rookie to lead All-Star voting</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Hill becomes first rookie to lead All-Star voting</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NBA-STARS 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES> (Eds: adds Western Conference team from 9th graf)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Grant Hill of the Detroit Pistons narrowly beat out Orlando Magic star Shaquille O'Neal to become the first rookie in National Basketball Association All-Star fan balloting to lead all players in votes received. </P>
<P> Hill, who has made a smooth transition from his superstar college days at Duke, had 1,289,585 votes to claim a starting forward spot and edge powerful center O'Neal by 26,134 votes in the final Eastern Conference returns announced Thursday. </P>
<P> Guards Magic Johnson (1980) and Isiah Thomas (1982) had the previous best showing among rookies. Each finished third. </P>
<P> Hill and O'Neal are joined in the Eastern Conference starting lineup by forward Scottie Pippen of the Chicago Bulls and guards Anfernee Hardaway of Orlando and Reggie Miller of Indiana. </P>
<P> The 45th annual NBA All-Star Game will be played at the America West Arena in Phoenix Sunday, Feb. 12. </P>
<P> Patrick Ewing of the New York Knicks finished second to O'Neal in the center voting with 728,331 votes. </P>
<P> Larry Johnson of the Charlotte Hornets was third among forwards with 698,931 votes followed by Chris Webber of the Washington Bullets (491,783). </P>
<P> On the Western Conference squad, forward Charles Barkley and guard Dan Majerle of the Phoenix Suns assume familiar roles in the starting line-up at the America West Arena. </P>
<P> Barkley led forwards with 1,046,105 votes, while Majerle led guards with 868,115 votes in the final returns. </P>
<P> Center Hakeem Olajuwon of the Houston Rockets, last season's NBA Most Valuable Player, forward Shawn Kemp of the Seattle SuperSonics and guard Latrell Sprewell of the Golden State Warriors were also voted starters for the West. </P>
<P> Olajuwon won a close race over David Robinson of the San Antonio Spurs. Olajuwon finished with 1,125,032 votes, 78,668 votes more than Robinson's total. </P>
<P> Kemp also received more than a million votes (1,027,451). Jamal Mashburn of Dallas (742,815) was third among Western forwards followed by Chris Mullin of Golden State (598,125). </P>
<P> Sprewell will be the first Warriors guard to start in an All-Star Game in 29 years since Guy Rodgers represented the San Francisco Warriors. Sprewell received 861,223 votes. </P>
<P> In the league's closest race for a starting position this season, Tim Hardaway of the Warriors finished in third place among West guards, just 7,439 behind Sprewell. </P>
<P> All-Star reserves will be announced Tuesday, Jan. 31. </P>
