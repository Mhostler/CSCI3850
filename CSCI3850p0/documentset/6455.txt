<TITLE> Heavy fighting in Philippines kills about 20</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Heavy fighting in Philippines kills about 20</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PHILIPPINES-FIGHTING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MANILA, May 11 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Philippine election and military officials said on Thursday about 20 people had been killed in heavy fighting between followers of a local politician and soldiers in the south of the country. </P>
<P> Troops overran the stronghold of a district occupied by followers of a local Moslem political leader on Jolo Island, 950 km (600 miles) south of Manila, on Wednesday, an election official told reporters in Manila. </P>
<P> At least 11 gunmen who support the influential Tulawie clan on Jolo have been killed in the fighting. The military said the number of those killed may have reached 20. </P>
<P> Some 13 marines have also been wounded in the clashes, which began on Monday when followers of the Tulawies opened fire on election officials delivering ballot boxes to the town for national elections. </P>
<P> A group of around 200-300 gunmen have fled from soldiers and marines who have launched an assault in the area. The attack was supported by helicopter gunships firing rockets at the gunmen. </P>
<P> At least 70 people have been killed since campaigning began, mostly on the troubled southern island of Mindanao where intense local rivalries have fuelled most of the violence. </P>
