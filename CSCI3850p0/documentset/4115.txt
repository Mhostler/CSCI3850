<TITLE> U.N. owed $3.6 b. at end of January</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.N. owed $3.6 b. at end of January</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-UN-DUES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>UNITED NATIONS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  United Nations members owed the organisation a whopping $3.6 billion at the end of January, a U.N. spokesman said Thursday. </P>
<P> Unpaid contributions to the regular budget amounted to $1.4 billion and another $2.2 billion was owed for peacekeeping operations. </P>
<P> As of January 31, only 19 of the U.N.'s 185 members had paid their 1995 regular budget assessments in full. They were: Australia, Canada, Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Kuwait, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Malaysia, Micronesia, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Pakistan, Singapore, Slovenia, Sri Lanka and Sweden. </P>
<P> By December 31, only 75 members had paid their 1994 regular budget dues in full. </P>
