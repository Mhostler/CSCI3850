<TITLE> Aids scandal French doctor released from jail</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Aids scandal French doctor released from jail</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AIDS-FRANCE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>PARIS, May 15 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Michel Garretta, the doctor serving the longest jail term in a scandal over AIDS-tainted blood transfusions, has been released from prison, spokeswoman for Paris's La Sante prison said on Monday. </P>
<P> Garretta, the former head of the national blood bank, was freed automatically for good behaviour after serving more than half of a four-year sentence imposed in 1992 for fraud and criminal negligence for distributing tainted blood products to hameophiliacs in the 1980s. </P>
