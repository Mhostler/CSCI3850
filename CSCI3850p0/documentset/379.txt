<TITLE> CBS, FOX MAY SWAP STATIONS - TIMES</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> CBS, FOX MAY SWAP STATIONS - TIMES</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-STATIONS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  CBS and Fox Broadcasting, a unit of News Corp Ltd, are cooperating on a possible deal that to exchange television stations, The New York Times reported in its Monday edition. </P>
<P> If the deal is successful, CBS will avoid losing a station in Detroit, the paper said. </P>
<P> Fox will gain a strong affiliate in Philadelphia if the deal is completed, the Times said.  The station swap would boost the prospects for programs on both networks this fall, the paper said. General Electric Co's NBC may be hurt by such a move, the paper said. </P>
<P> CBS would give the station it owns in Philadelphia, WCAU, to (New World Communications Group Inc), which is partly owned by Fox, the paper said. </P>
<P> In return, CBS would get WJBK in Detroit, a station which was expected to switch to Fox, from New World. </P>
<P> Fox outbid CBS to gain a television contract with the National Football League and gained eight CBS affiliates plus four other stations when it made an equity investment in New World. </P>
<P> CBS previously struck a deal with Westinghouse Electric Corp's Group W unit, which gives CBS another station in Philadelphia, the paper said. The station, WKYW, is currently an NBC affiliate. </P>
<P> Group W and CBS will jointly own the station under the deal, the paper said. </P>
