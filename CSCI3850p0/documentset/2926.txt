<TITLE> Country singer Jackson signs with Warner-Chappell</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Country singer Jackson signs with Warner-Chappell</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-JACKSON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NASHVILLE, Tenn.(Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Country singer Alan Jackson reached a $10 million publishing deal Wednesday with Warner-Chappell Music. </P>
<P> The deal covers songs Jackson has written, including some turned out while he was a struggling unknown. The agreement tops the $3 million deal that veteran singer-writer Merle Haggard reached last year for publishing rights to his hits. </P>
<P> Jackson, a lanky native of Newton, Ga., has written such smash hits as ``Chattahoochee'', ``Don't Rock the Jukebox'' and ''Here in the Real World.'' </P>
<P> Also this week in the country music mecca of Nashville, multimillionaire and country music star Garth Brooks threw a lavish party to celebrate the sale of more than 11 million copies of his album ``No Fences.'' </P>
