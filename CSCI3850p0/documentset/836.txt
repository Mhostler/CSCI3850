<AUTHOR>     By Cindy West </AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> Citing security concerns, Linda Taggart, director of the clinic, said she would neither identify the doctor nor say where he is from. Pensacola police said they would provide protection to the doctor but also declined to give details. </P>
<P> The Ladies Center typically has hired off-duty police officers to provide security for its staff and patients. </P>
<P> After Britton, 69, and his clinic escort James Barrett, 74, were shot and killed at the center, Pensacolt until next week. </P>
<P> Paul Hill, 40, of Pensacola, who is charged with murdering Britton and Barrett and wounding Barrett's wife, June, is scheduled to be arraigned Thursday on two counts of first-degree murder, one count of attempted first-degree murder and shooting into an occupied vehicle. </P>
<P> Hill, a former Presbyterian minister and a militant opponent of abortion, had characterized the murder of abortion doctors as justifiable homicide. </P>
<P> An Escambia Circuit judge said in a court memo that the defense attorney and prosecutor agreed that Hill's trial, scheduled for January 30, 1995 and expected to last two weeks, would not be delayed beyond that time. </P>
<P> If convicted of first-degree murder, Hill could receive either the death penalty or life in prison. </P>
<P> Britton was the second doctor who performed abortions to be murdered in Pensacola. Dr David Gunn was shot and killed at the Florida panhandle city's other abortion clinic in March 1993. </P>
