<TITLE> Springsteen, Sheryl Crow win Grammys</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Springsteen, Sheryl Crow win Grammys</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-GRAMMYS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR>     By Michael Miller </AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LOS ANGELES (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Pop newcomer Sheryl Crow and veteran rocker Bruce Springsteen were among the early winners Wednesday as the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences presented its 37th Annual Grammy Awards. </P>
<P> Crow, nominated in five categories, won her first Grammy ever for Best New Artist. </P>
<P> Springsteen, who was also up for five awards, took the Grammys for Song of the Year and Best Rock Performance by a Male for ``Streets of Philadelphia,'' the theme song to the Oscar-winning AIDS movie, ``Philadelphia,'' which brought Tom Hanks an Academy Award for Actor of the Year last year. </P>
<P> The female trio Salt-n-Pepa took the Grammy for for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group with their song, ``None of Your Business''/''Whatta Man.'' </P>
<P> Crow, who grew up in Missouri, beat out Ace of Base, Counting Crows, Crash Test Dummies and Green Day for Best New Artist. </P>
<P> But her record, ``All I Wanna Do,'' from her debut album ''Tuesday Night Music Club,'' was not successful in the Song of the Year Category, losing to Springfield's ``Philadelphia.'' </P>
<P> Others nominated in that category were ``Can You Feel the Love Tonight'' and ``Circle of Life,'' both from the movie, ''The Lion King,'' and written and sung by Elton John, and ``I Swear'' from All 4 One. </P>
<P> Crow was also nominated for Best Female Vocal Rock Performance for ``I'm Gonna be a Wheel Someday,'' and Best Female Pop Vocal Performance for ``All I Wanna Do,'' as well as Record of the Year for the same song. </P>
<P> Those awards were due later in the evening. </P>
<P> Springsteen and ``Streets of Philadelphia,'' were also nominated for Record of the Year, Best Rock Song and Best Song Written for Motion Picture of Television, which were also to be announced later in the evening. </P>
<P> Mary Chapin Carpenter won the Grammy for Best Country Album for ``Stones in the Road,'' beating out Asleep at the Wheel's ''Tribute to the Music of Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys,'' Vince Gill's ``When Love Finds You,'' Reba McEntire's ``Read My Mind,'' and ``The Song Remembers When,'' by Trisha Yearwood. </P>
