<TITLE> Quake hits western Texas, southeastern New Mexico</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Quake hits western Texas, southeastern New Mexico</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-TEXAS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>GOLDEN, Colo.(Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  An earthquake measuring 5.6 on the open-ended Richter scale shook western Texas and parts of southeastern New Mexico Friday, the National Earthquake Information Center said. </P>
<P> The epicenter of the quake was in Alpine, Texas, east of the Mexican border in the southern part of the state. </P>
<P> The quake occurred at 7:33 pm CDT (8:33 EDT). Stuart Koyanagi of the center said the quake was the largest ever detected in that area. </P>
<P> CNN reported that the quake caused no injuries but jolted residents in their homes. </P>
