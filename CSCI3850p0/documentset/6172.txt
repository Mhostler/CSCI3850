<TITLE> Death toll hits three in Russian train accident</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Death toll hits three in Russian train accident</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CIS-RUSSIA-TRAIN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW, May 1 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A third person has died after an accident in Siberia in which a freight train carrying 35 tanks of crude oil collided with a mechanical road-digger and burst into flames, Russia's Itar-Tass news agency said on Monday. </P>
<P> Tass quoted the Ministry for Emergency Situations as saying the driver of the road-digger had died from injuries received in Sunday's incident near a station close to the city of Chita, 200 km (125 miles) north of the Mongolian border. </P>
<P> The train driver and his assistant had already been reported killed after 10 tankloads of crude oil burst caught fire when the train left the rails. Dozens of firefighters, emergency workers and soldiers took five hours to put out the blaze. </P>
<P> Interfax news agency quoted the ministry as saying some Trans-Siberian rail traffic interrupted by the accident would restart on Monday. </P>
