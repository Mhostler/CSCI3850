<TITLE> Japan cult leader Asahara is arrested</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Japan cult leader Asahara is arrested</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-JAPAN-SUBWAY-ARREST 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>    (Updates with details)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>KAMIKU ISSHIKI, Japan, May 16 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Doomsday cult leader Shoko Asahara was arrested on Tuesday hours after massive raids were launched across Japan to round up sect followers linked to the Tokyo subway poison gas attack. </P>
<P> ``Police have arrested Chizuo Matsumoto, known as Shoko Asahara,'' Tokyo police Superintendent Yukihiko Inoue told a nationally televised news conference. </P>
<P> Asahara, dressed in a white hooded garment, was led out of the cult's headquarters in this farming village at the foot of Mount Fuji to a police car. </P>
<P> He was arrested about four hours after massive police raids started against cult locations to round up followers linked to the March 20 sarin nerve gas attack on the Tokyo subway system which killed 12 and injured more than 5,000. </P>
<P> Police said Asahara was meditating alone on the third floor of one of the complex's buildings when police discovered him. </P>
<P> Fuji Television said Asahara told police ``I understand'' when investigators read him the arrest warrant. </P>
