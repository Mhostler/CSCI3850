<TITLE> Young Britons' cannabis use triples in three years</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Young Britons' cannabis use triples in three years</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BRITAIN-CANNABIS (EMBARGOED) </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES>(Release at 7 p.m. EDT)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>LONDON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The number of 15 and 16-year-old British boys who have smoked cannabis has tripled in the past three years and one third now say they have used the illegal drug, a nationwide survey showed Monday. </P>
<P> More than 48,000 children aged between 11 and 16 were questioned by researchers from Exeter University for a BBC television program. </P>
<P> They found 32.9 percent of boys aged 15 and 16, and 27.3 percent of girls admitted having smoked cannabis in 1994. </P>
<P> This compared with 11.3 percent of boys and 8.9 percent of girls in 1991, the BBC said in a statement. </P>
