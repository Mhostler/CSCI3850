<TITLE> U.N. summit ends, adopts action plan on poverty</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.N. summit ends, adopts action plan on poverty</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SOCIAL-END </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>COPENHAGEN, Denmark (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A U.N. social summit adopted early Monday an action plan that pledged to eradicate poverty, social injustice and unemployment while giving more help to the world's developing countries. </P>
<P> ``We commit ourselves to the goal of eradicating poverty in the world,'' said the plan, approved by leaders and delegates from 182 countries at a final televised session of what has been billed as the biggest summit in history. </P>
<P> Danish Prime Minister Poul Nyrup Rasmussen, in a statement closing the summit, said the United Nations would review progress on its summit commitments in the year 2000. ``I would like, when we meet five years hence, to look back to this summit of hope ... as a summit of fulfilled expectations,'' he said. </P>
<P></P>
<P> ``We make this solemn commitment on the eve of the fiftieth anniversary of the United Nations and with a determination to capture the unique possibilities offered by the end of the Cold War to promote social development and social justice ... </P>
<P> ``We can continue to hold the trust of the people of the world only if we make their needs our priority.'' </P>
