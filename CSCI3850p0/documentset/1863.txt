<SUBJECT> BC-IRAQ-KUWAIT-USA-FORCES 1STLD -2 WASHINGTON </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> The official, who asked not to be identified, said that ''less than 15,000'' U.S. Army and Marine troops had been put on alert in the United States, but that no decision had been made to send them to Kuwait yet. </P>
<P> The official told reporters that the George Washington was headed toward the Red Sea from the Adriatic and that as many as 12 fully-loaded U.S. military cargo ships now in the Indian Ocean and Pacific had been ordered either to move toward Iraq or stand by for orders. </P>
<P> Four U.S. warships with a force of 2,000 Marines will also be moved from the southern Persian Gulf and moved into the northern Gulf off Kuwait within a day or so, the official said. </P>
<P> A full Iraqi division of some 10,000 troops from President Saddam Hussein's elite Republic Guard have been moved recently into southern Iraq to join several other divisions, bringing Iraqi forces in the area to the range of 40,000 to 50,000, the official said. </P>
