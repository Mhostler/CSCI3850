<TITLE> Worry about terror attacks runs high in U.S.-poll</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Worry about terror attacks runs high in U.S.-poll</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BLAST-POLL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Public fears of further terror bombings in the United States are running high after the devastating attack on a U.S. government building in Oklahoma City where at least 53 people were killed, an ABC News poll Thursday said. </P>
<P> The poll of 545 adults conducted by telephone Thursday said 78 percent of those questioned expressed worry there will be more major terrorist attacks in the country. </P>
<P> Sixty-two percent said they have little or no confidence the government can prevent such attacks, up 21 percent from a poll conducted nearly two years ago after the World Trade Center bombing in New York. </P>
<P> The poll found majority support for extraordinary measures to suppress terrorism. Fifty-nine percent said they are ''willing to give up some of the liberties we have in this country'' in order to crack down on terrorism. </P>
<P> The poll has a margin of error of five percentage points. </P>
