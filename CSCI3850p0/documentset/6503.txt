<P> Boutros-Ghali will report to the Security Council on his return to New York, the statement said. </P>
<P> Boutros-Ghali and his aides had conferred for two and a half hours in a Paris hotel on the deterioration of the situation in Croatia, the escalation of fighting following the expiration of a cease-fire May 1 and increasing attacks on U.N. peacekeeping soldiers, the statement said. </P>
<P> Participating in the discussions were Yasushi Akashi, Chief U.N. representative in the Balkans, U.N. mediator Thorvald Stoltenberg, French Gen. Bernard Janvier, the overall U.N. commander in former Yugoslavia, and British Gen. Rupert Smith, commander in Bosnia. </P>
<P> Akashi had said before their talks that he hoped Boutros-Ghali would give ``some new directions'' about how to tackle worsening conditions in Bosnia for the U.N. troops after three years of war. </P>
