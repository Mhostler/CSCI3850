<TITLE> Pakistani activist's death sparks violence</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pakistani activist's death sparks violence</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PAKISTAN-VIOLENCE (PICTURE) </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KARACHI, Pakistan, April 9 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Supporters of the Mohajir National Movement (MQM) set shops and at least four buses on fire in the southern port city of Karachi on Sunday in protest at the death of an MQM activist, witnesses said. </P>
<P> Police said the MQM activist was killed during a series of clashes between rival MQM factions and police on Saturday, when a total of eight people, two of them policemen, were shot dead. </P>
<P> A provincial deputy of the MQM, who asked not to be named, accused police of killing the activist in cold blood. </P>
<P> A police spokesmen said gunmen ambushed a police patrol in District East late on Saturday night, killing two policemen. At least two of the attackers were wounded by return fire. </P>
<P> The deaths brought the number of security personnel killed since the army withdrew from Karachi in late November to 52, 44 policemen, seven rangers and one soldier. </P>
<P> The spokesman said a day of gunfire in District East had begun when rival MQM factions clashed. The feuding MQM militants turned their guns on police when they tried to intervene. </P>
<P> The gunmen attacked a police station and terrified local residents by firing in the air and at shops. One person was killed sitting in front of a shop. His companion was critically wounded. </P>
<P> Gunmen shooting at random from cars killed four people in other parts of the city, including two boys aged 10 and 14, who had been playing in the street. </P>
<P> A private security guard at a factory in the industrial District West of Karachi was killed by unidentified assailants who took his rifle before escaping. </P>
<P> Hospital doctors said they had received a body of an unidentified person with bullet wounds from District East. </P>
<P> At least 385 people have died so far this year in factional, ethnic and sectarian violence in Karachi, where 800 people were killed in 1994. </P>
