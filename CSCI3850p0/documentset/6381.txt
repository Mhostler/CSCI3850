<TITLE> U.S. tactician suggests expectant Kiwi father go home</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> U.S. tactician suggests expectant Kiwi father go home</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-YACHTING-AMERICAS-BABY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SAN DIEGO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Team New Zealand tactician Brad Butterworth's wife was due to give birth within a couple hours of the finish of Race 2 of the America's Cup finals Monday. </P>
<P> After a lopsided defeat by the Kiwis, his Stars & Stripes counterpart suggested it was his husbandly duty to go home. </P>
<P> At a press conference after Monday's race, Butterworth said he had not yet heard whether his wife Marie had given birth back in Auckland. </P>
<P> ``Not yet. Not that I know of. It's imminent, though,'' said Butterworth. </P>
<P> Suggested Stars & Stripes tactician Tom Whidden, whose boat finished a devastating 4 minutes, 14 seconds back to fall behind 0-2 in the best-of-nine series: ``Maybe you should go home, Brad.'' </P>
