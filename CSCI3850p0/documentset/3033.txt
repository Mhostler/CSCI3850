<TITLE> Plane makes emergency landing on Long Island</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Plane makes emergency landing on Long Island</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-LANDING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Engine trouble forced a Business Express commuter plane, which airport officials said was traveling from Boston to Philadelphia, to make an emergency landing at a Long Island, N.Y. airport Tuesday, the FAA said. </P>
<P> Federal Aviation Administration spokeswoman Joan Brown said Business Express Flight 579, a SAAB Fairchild 340 twin engine turbo prop carrying 19 passengers, landed at Islip's MacArthur Airport at 3:09 p.m., reporting that its No. 2 engine was out. </P>
<P> Business Express is a subsidiary of Delta Airlines. </P>
<P> The flight was due to land in Philadelphia at 3:35 p.m., according to airport officials there. </P>
<P> FAA officials said the emergency landing was routine. </P>
<P> The Business Express manager at the Islip airport refused to comment and no one was available to answer questions at the company's headquarters in Portsmouth, New Hampshire. </P>
<P> A spate of recent crashes, collisions and other problems has plagued the commuter airline industry, riveting national attention to the industry's safety standards and performance. </P>
