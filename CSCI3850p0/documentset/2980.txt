<TITLE> Pro-Kurdish newspaper bombed in Istanbul, one killed</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Pro-Kurdish newspaper bombed in Istanbul, one killed</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-TURKEY-BOMB </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ANKARA, Turkey (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A bomb exploded at the pro-Kurdish daily Ozgur Ulke's office in Istanbul Saturday, destroying the building and killing one man, Anatolian news agency said. </P>
<P> Nineteen 19 people were wounded, it said. </P>
<P> The four-story building housed the newspaper's main editorial office. No one has claimed responsibility for the pre-dawn bombing. </P>
<P> Ozgur Ulke has close ties to the Kurdistan Workers Party (PKK), which has been fighting for an independent state for Turkish Kurds for past decade. It publishes a column by PKK leader Abdullah Ocalan. </P>
<P> The government has cracked down hard on writers and journalists it perceives as sympathetic to the PKK cause. </P>
