<TITLE> EARTHQUAKE ROCKS JAPAN</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> EARTHQUAKE ROCKS JAPAN</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-JAPAN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TOKYO (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  An earthquake measuring 5.5 on the Richter scale rocked a wide area of northeastern Japan at about 6:07 p.m. (5:07 a.m. EDT) Sunday, the Meteorological Agency said. </P>
<P> There were no reports of damages or casualties. </P>
<P> The quake measured four on the Japanese scale of seven in Ofunato, Iwate Prefecture, where the jolt was strongest. </P>
<P> The epicenter was located 25 miles under the sea off Miyagi prefecture, northern Japan, the agency said. </P>
