<TITLE> Education Secretary to have prostrate removed</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Education Secretary to have prostrate removed</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-RILEY-SURGERY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Education Secretary Richard Riley, diagnosed recently with prostate cancer, will have his prostate gland removed Tuesday, the Education Department said in a statement late Monday. </P>
<P> Riley, 61, was expected to recover fully and return to work in a few weeks, the department said. The secretary planned to keep in close touch with senior staff and participate in some meetings electronically. </P>
<P> Riley's surgery will take place at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore, the leading center for treatment of prostate cancer. His condition was discovered during a routine physical. </P>
