<TITLE> Spousal abuse relevant to Simpson murder case: poll</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Spousal abuse relevant to Simpson murder case: poll</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SIMPSON-POLL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW YORK (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Six out of 10 Americans polled said the reported episodes and allegations of spousal abuse in O.J. Simpson's marriage are relevant to the charge he murdered his ex-wife and should be presented to the jury, a Newsweek survey released Saturday found. </P>
<P> The poll, which appears in the issue available on newsstands Monday, also found that 75 percent of those asked thought the courts and legal system have been fair so far to the former football great. </P>
<P> And asked whether they thought the jury would convict Simpson of the double homicide, 38 percent of those surveyed said he would be found guilty, 35 percent said not guilty, and 27 percent were unsure. </P>
<P> The telephone survey of 757, conducted Thursday and Friday, had a four point margin of error. </P>
