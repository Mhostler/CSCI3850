<TITLE> Wartime bombs defused in Germany, 15,000 evacuated</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Wartime bombs defused in Germany, 15,000 evacuated</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-GERMANY-BOMB </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LUDWIGSHAFEN, Germany (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  More than 15,000 people were evacuated from their homes after unexploded World War Two bombs were found buried in two German towns, city officials said Saturday. </P>
<P> Experts in Ludwigshafen defused a British aerial bomb containing about 2,800 lbs of explosives discovered in a city park Friday. </P>
<P> In the small Bavarian town of Freystadt, building workers found a smaller American bomb which was safely defused. </P>
<P> The Ludwigshafen bomb was the second in three weeks to be discovered in the city, a chemical industry center frequently targeted by Allied planes during the war. The first bomb was found in the same park Oct. 19. </P>
<P> Horst Lentz, head of the bomb disposal squad, told reporters photos taken by Allied pilots suggested more bombs could be buried there. </P>
<P> Three people were killed and 17 injured in Berlin in September when work on a building site set off a bomb. </P>
