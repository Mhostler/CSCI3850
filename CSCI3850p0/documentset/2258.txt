<TITLE> Ringleader pleads guilty in phone fraud-paper</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Ringleader pleads guilty in phone fraud-paper</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PHONE-FRAUD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The leader of an international ring of computer hackers pleaded guilty on Wednesday to stealing thousands of telephone calling card numbers and using them to make up to $140 million worth of calls, the Washington Post reported on Thursday. </P>
<P> The newspaper quoted U.S. officials as saying Max Louarn, of Majorca, Spain, helped run one of the largest and more sophisticated phone fraud ever. </P>
<P> Louarn, 22, was arrested last month. He pleaded guilty to charges of conspiracy and wire fraud. </P>
<P> The paper said as many as 140,000 calling card numbers were stolen by a network of thieves operating in the United States, England and Spain. The numbers were issued by AT&T Corp, GTE Corp, Bell Atlantic and MCI Communications Corp. </P>
