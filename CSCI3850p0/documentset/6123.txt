<TITLE> House surrounded after Quebec police chief shot dead</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> House surrounded after Quebec police chief shot dead</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CANADA-HOSTAGE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>STE-MARIE-DE-BEAUCE, Quebec (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A provincial police squad surrounded a house Friday after the police chief in this town was shot dead by a man believed to be holed up in the house. </P>
<P> Witnesses told Reuters Denis Nadeau, 60, the chief of police in this community 48 miles south of Quebec City for 35 years, was shot as he approached the house early Friday. Police officials said his alleged assailant, Clement Mercier, was still in the house and was holding a 10-year-old girl hostage. </P>
<P> Officials said the girl is the daughter of Mercier's common law wife, who escaped from the house shortly after the shooting and was being treated in a local hospital for shock. </P>
<P> Members of the tactical squad of the Surete du Quebec surrounded the house near the center of the town, which has a population of about 11,000. Police used an armored vehicle to recover the police chief's body, which lay outside for about two hours. </P>
<P> Police spokesman Camille Gagnon told Reuters the tactical squad was trying to communicate with Mercier. </P>
