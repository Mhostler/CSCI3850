<TITLE> Police say 15 known dead in NZ cliff fall</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Police say 15 known dead in NZ cliff fall</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NEWZEALAND-FALL-TOLL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WELLINGTON, April 28 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Police said on Friday that 15 people were known to have fallen to their deaths when a cliffside viewing platform collapsed on New Zealand's West Coast. </P>
<P> ``So far there are 15 known dead and they will be taken to an emergency mortuary at Greymouth hospital for identification,'' a police statement said. </P>
<P> Four injured people had been taken to hospital and five survived with minor injuries, the statement said. </P>
<P> It said the victims fell 100 feet (30 metres) to their deaths from a viewing platform which gave way. It was not known how many people were standing on the platform at the time. </P>
<P> Senior Sergeant Phil Deazley of Greymouth police said resources were stretched, the accident scene was outside radio range and no more details were immediately available. </P>
