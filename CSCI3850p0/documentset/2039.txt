<SUBJECT> BC-HAITI-ARISTIDE-DEPARTURE -2 ANDREWS AIR FORCE BASE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> ``Let's celebrate this reconciliation,'' Aristide told supporters at the airport. ``Let's go to Haiti to continue celebrating this wonderful day of peace, democracy, respect. And, of course, it will be more than one day. It will be for some months and for some years.'' </P>
<P> In a final word of thanks to President Clinton, Aristide said: ``Because of your leadership, President Clinton, you helped us (in) restoring democracy. Be assured on our side, we will keep our commitment. You will succeed. We will succeed, and the world will celebrate this wonderful way to build a new world order.'' </P>
<P> Said Christopher: ``Today, the people of Haiti will resume their journey to freedom and reconstruction in dignity and peace ... When President Aristide lands, it will be a glorious moment for the Haitian people.'' </P>
<P> Tens of thousands of Haitians were expected to take to the streets to welcome back the champion of Haiti's poor masses who was ousted in a September 1991 coup. U.S. troops entered Haiti September 19 under an agreement with Haitian military officials who have since relinquished power and left the country. </P>
