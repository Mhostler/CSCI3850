<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> The official, who asked not to be identified, said a formal announcement of Carter's mission, was expected later Saturday from the Carter Center in Atlanta, Georgia. </P>
<P> In sketching Carter's expected itinerary, he said the former president would take a commerical flight Saturday evening to Frankfurt where he would be briefed by U.S. Ambassador to Germany Charles Redman, formerly America's special envoy to the ex-Yugoslavia. </P>
<P> From Frankfurt, the official said, a U.S. military aircraft would fly Carter to Zagreb, Croatia, where he was expected to meet Croatian President Franjo Tudjman and, possibly, Bosnian Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic. </P>
<P> An aircraft of the U.N. protection force in the former Yugoslavia, UNPROFOR, would then fly him to Sarajevo where he would arrive on Sunday evening or Monday morning. the official said. </P>
