<TITLE> Egypt, Syria, Saudi Arabia end two-day summit</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Egypt, Syria, Saudi Arabia end two-day summit</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MIDEAST-SUMMIT-END </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ALEXANDRIA, Egypt (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Leaders of Egypt, Syria and Saudi Arabia ended a two-day summit Thursday, endorsing Syria's position in its deadlocked peace talks with Israel and calling on Iraq to fully implement U.N. resolutions. </P>
<P> Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak, Syrian President Hafez al-Assad and Saudi Arabia's King Fahd also urged the world community to work to remove obstacles that they said Israel placed in the path of the peace process. </P>
<P> The leaders, who held lengthy discussions Wednesday and met briefly Thursday, also said they wanted the Middle East to be free of weapons of mass destruction, particularly nuclear weapons. </P>
<P> In a statement given to reporters at the end of the summit, they also said peace in the Middle East must be based on U.N. resolutions calling for a withdrawal of Israeli troops from occupied Arab lands and on the principle of land for peace. </P>
