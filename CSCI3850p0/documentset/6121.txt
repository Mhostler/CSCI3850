<TITLE> Nearly 60 leaders due at London WWII events</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Nearly 60 leaders due at London WWII events</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-WORLDWAR-BRITAIN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LONDON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Nearly 60 leaders will attend next week's British ceremonies marking the 50th anniversary of the end of World War II, the government said Friday. </P>
<P> From Albania to Uzbekistan, presidents, prime ministers and princes will pour into London for commemorations next weekend leading up to VE day on May 8. </P>
<P> Events include a May 6 reception hosted by Prime Minister John Major, followed on May 7 by a service at St Paul's Cathedral, lunch at Buckingham Palace and a Hyde Park ceremony. </P>
<P> The government, which refused to release the full list of invitees as tensions still linger half a century after the fighting stopped, said more acceptances were expected. </P>
<P> As previosuly announced, President Clinton, who will be in Moscow on VE Day, will not be coming to London. Vice President Al Gore will come instead. </P>
<P> Japan's leaders were not invited, although German Chancellor Helmut Kohl and President Roman Herzog will attend. </P>
<P> Among others attending are: French President Francois Mitterrand, Bosnian President Alija Izetbegovic, New Zealand Prime Minister Jim Bolger, Croatian President Franjo Tudjman, Israeli President Ezer Weizman and Canadian Prime Minister Jean Chretien. </P>
