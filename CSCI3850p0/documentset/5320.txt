<TITLE> Egyptian police kill five suspected militants</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Egyptian police kill five suspected militants</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-MILITANTS 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES> (Adds farmer killed, updates death toll in three years)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>ASSIUT, Egypt, March 30 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Police shot dead five men thought to be Moslem militants in southern Egypt on Thursday morning, security sources said. </P>
<P> Police said the men were killed in an exchange of fire after police went to arrest them at a house in the village of Abu Khuzam, in the Nile valley 280 km (175 miles) south of Cairo, they said. There were no police casualties. </P>
<P> The government says its policy is to capture militants alive but human rights groups said police shoot to kill, without giving militants a chance to surrender. </P>
<P> The sources said police were identifying the dead but believed them to be members of the Gama'a al-Islamiya, the largest militant group fighting to overthrow the government and set up a strict Islamic state. </P>
<P> Also on Thursday, suspected members of al-Gamaa killed a 45-year-old farmer for alleged cooperation with the police. </P>
<P> Police said suspected militants shot dead Shehata Fahmy Sakran in the market of a village near Minya, 260 km (160 miles) south of Cairo. A woman at the scene was wounded, police added. </P>
<P> More than 700 people have been killed in political violence since the Gama'a took up arms three years ago. </P>
