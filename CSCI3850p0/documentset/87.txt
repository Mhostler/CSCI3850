<TITLE> ISRAELI, PLO LEADERS START PARIS TALKS</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> ISRAELI, PLO LEADERS START PARIS TALKS</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-MIDEAST-TALKS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>PARIS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin and PLO Chairman Yasser Arafat began talks in Paris Wednesday to try to agree on the next stage of Palestinian self-rule. </P>
<P> Rabin, accompanied by his Foreign Minister Shimon Peres, met Arafat at the Kleber conference center in central Paris. </P>
<P> The three leaders scheduled a one-hour working session followed by separate news conferences. </P>
<P> They will later drive to the headquarters of the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization to receive its $150,000 peace prize. </P>
<P> UNESCO awarded them the prize, named after the late Ivory Coast president Felix Houphouet Boigny, for their September 1993 mutual recognition agreement which ended decades of Israeli-Palestinian enmity. </P>
