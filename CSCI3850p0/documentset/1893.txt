<TITLE> Heavy rocketing kills 17 in Kabul - radio</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Heavy rocketing kills 17 in Kabul - radio</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AFGHAN-ROCKETS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ISLAMABAD (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Opponents of Afghan President Burhanuddin Rabbani rained rockets on Kabul Sunday, killing 17 people and injuring 38, the official Kabul Radio said. </P>
<P> The broadcast, monitored in Islamabad, said forces loyal to Prime Minister Gulbuddin Hekmatyar and northern warlord General Abdul Rashid Dostum fired 61 rockets at residential areas. </P>
<P> The Rabbani-controlled radio said several houses were damaged and four cars burned. </P>
<P> It said Dostum's jets had bombed the northern town of Kunduz 35 times in the past two days, causing severe damage. There was no word on casualties and no independent confirmation. </P>
<P> Last week Rabbani invited a U.N. mission to visit Kabul to discuss how to bring peace to the war-shattered country. </P>
<P> A group of independent Afghan personalities which has been holding talks with U.N. officials in the western Pakistani city of Quetta last week asked U.N. special envoy Mahmoud Mestiri to invite warring Afghan leaders to join their discussions. </P>
