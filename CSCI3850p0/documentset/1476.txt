<P> He said U.S. Army Lieutenant General Henry Shelton, commander of the American force, would go to Haiti Monday morning to discuss details of the troop movement with Haitian military leader Lieutenant General Raoul Cedras. </P>
<P> ``I expect the flow to start later on today,'' said Perry. ''They will go in gradually. Within a week or so we expect 14,000 to 15,000'' U.S. troops in Haiti, Perry added. </P>
<P> Asked if relatively small groups of American troops going in early might be at risk, he replied: ``Each group that is in will be capable of defending itself, both in terms of size and in terms of arms.'' </P>
<P> Perry said U.S. 82nd Airborne Division paratroopers, who were recalled after taking off from Pope Air Force Base, North Carolina, for an invasion late Sunday, were halfway to Haiti when they were recalled after an 11th agreement between U.S. and Haitian negotiators in Port-au-Prince to avoid an invasion. </P>
<P> ``The paratroopers were scheduled to go in at one minute after midnight,'' he said. </P>
<P> Defense officials told Reuters earier that helicopters with U.S. troops had also taken off from the aircraft carrier America off Haiti and were recalled when the agreement was reached. Big AWACS early-warning radar planes were also in the air near Haiti for the invasion. </P>
