<TITLE> At least 20 killed as Chinese train hits bus</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> At least 20 killed as Chinese train hits bus</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-ACCIDENT 2NDLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BEIJING (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A train slammed into a bus carrying workers to a factory in China's capital Beijing Thursday, killing at least 20 as it split the bus in half. </P>
<P> A Reuter reporter on the scene saw the two halves of the bus on either side of the track and scores of police trying to keep away curious onlookers. </P>
<P> Occupants of the bus were thrown in all directions by the impact of the crash. </P>
<P> The Beijing Neiranji Factory was just learning details of the tragedy in the southeast corner of the capital, which occurred during the morning rush hour as the laborers were going to their jobs, workers at the factory said by telephone. </P>
<P> They said at least 20 people had been killed and up to 80 injured. </P>
<P> Railway Ministry officials declined to comment on details, although municipal health workers confirmed there were many deaths. </P>
<P> Officials at one hospital, the Chui Yang Liu, said they had seen 46 injured people, 21 of whom were in serious condition. </P>
<P> They said victims had been sent to many hospitals around the city, making a toll assessment difficult. </P>
<P> It was not known if there were any foreigners involved. </P>
<P> Police at the scene confiscated the film from two television reporters who tried to videotape the carnage, witnesses said. </P>
<P> The accident was on one of the main rail corridors leading into the capital. </P>
