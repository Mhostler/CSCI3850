<TITLE> South Asian nations urge global ban on N-weapon use</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> South Asian nations urge global ban on N-weapon use</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-INDIA-SAARC </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>NEW DELHI, India (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The seven-member South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation plans to call for a world ban on the use of nuclear arms. </P>
<P></P>
<P> ``The proposed convention on nuclear weapons should be negotiated by the conference on disarmament which should also undertake discussions for complete elimination of all nuclear weapons within a time-bound framework.'' </P>
<P> Western nations are worried that the region faces a nuclear threat, arguing that India and Pakistan are both capable of building nuclear weapons fairly quickly. </P>
<P> Pakistan President Farooq Leghari raised the issue Tuesday, seeking a regional pact on nuclear weapons, but there was no public response from Indian Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao. </P>
<P> The association is made up of India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan and the Maldives. </P>
