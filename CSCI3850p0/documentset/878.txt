<TITLE> NATO FOREIGN MINISTERS MEET IN BRUSSELS</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> NATO FOREIGN MINISTERS MEET IN BRUSSELS</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-NATO-MEETING </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BRUSSELS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Secretary of State Warren Christopher met his French, British and German counterparts in Brussels Friday to discuss Bosnia and the threat posed by nuclear smuggling, officials said. </P>
<P> The NATO ministers were in Brussels to attend a memorial service for Secretary General Manfred Woerner, who died a week ago at the age of 59 after a long fight against cancer. </P>
<P> Before the service, British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd, French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe and German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel arrived at the hotel in central Brussels where Christopher was staying. </P>
<P> ``There is no shortage of subjects to discuss,'' Juppe told Reuters on arrival. </P>
<P> He said the main topic would probably be Bosnia. </P>
<P> Christopher's spokeswoman said he had discussed the recent alarming rash of nuclear smuggling cases and other issues with NATO Deputy Secretary General Sergio Balanzino Thursday. </P>
<P> ``It came up in general terms,'' she said. </P>
