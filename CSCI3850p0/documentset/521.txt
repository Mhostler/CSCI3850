<TITLE> ANOTHER PLANE CRASH IN STRATFORD</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> ANOTHER PLANE CRASH IN STRATFORD</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-USA-CRASH </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>STRATFORD, Conn.(Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A plane has crashed during takeoff at Sikorsky Memorial Airport in Stratford, less than four months after an accident that killed eight people. </P>
<P> The single engine Piper lost power during takeoff, crashing Sunday at 2.55 p.m. EDT. No one was injured and the passengers away from the accident, officials said Monday. </P>
<P> Four members of a Norwalk family were aboard. </P>
<P> ``They feel lucky,'' said Bridgeport Assistant Fire Chief Wallace Thomas. </P>
<P> The pilot, William Krummel of Norwalk, was heading to Provincetown, Massachusetts, with his wife, Regina, a son and a daughter. </P>
<P> In April, a plane returning in heavy fog on a chartered flight from Atlantic City, New Jersey, overshot the runway and crashed. Eight people were killed in what was the airport's worst accident. </P>
