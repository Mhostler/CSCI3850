<TITLE> Safety unit sets date to weigh cause of USAir crash</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Safety unit sets date to weigh cause of USAir crash</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-AVIATION-SAFETY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The National Transportation Safety Board will meet April 4 to assess the causes of a USAir crash near Charlotte, N.C., last July 2 that killed 37 people and injured 20, the board said Wednesday. </P>
<P> Board investigators conducted hearings into the crash of the DC-9 in Charlotte last Septmber and are to bring their findings to the independent board. </P>
<P> Early indications are that windshear from squally weather played a role in the crash. </P>
<P> In addition to windshear, the board will consider other possible causes, including overall weather factors, aircraft equipment and pilot and controller conduct. </P>
<P> Typically, the board determines the facts of the crashes, sets out the probable causes and then make recommendations, if necessary, to the Federal Aviation Administration to try to prevent similar incidents. </P>
