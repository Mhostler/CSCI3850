<TITLE> UGANDA WELCOMES REBEL CAPTURE OF RWANDAN CAPITAL</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> UGANDA WELCOMES REBEL CAPTURE OF RWANDAN CAPITAL</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-RWANDA-UGANDA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>KAMPALA, Uganda (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni welcomed the capture by rebels of Rwanda's capital Kigali Monday as a major step toward the formation of a broad-based government. ment and end this mayhem. </P>
<P> ``I think their victory will bring a balance in the politics of that country and bring a definite end to its problems.'' </P>
<P> The RPF, dominated by the minority Tutsi tribe, invaded from Uganda in 1990. Uganda has denied accusations by the Hutu interim government that it backs the rebels with arms and troops. </P>
<P> ``It is wrong, as I have always said, to presume that people from (the West) are the best placed to find a solution to the problems in Rwanda,'' Museveni said in apparent reference to the French. </P>
