<P> But Christopher seemed equally upset over the U.N.'s refusal to override the Serbs. ``I'm going to have something to say to the United Nations officials about their decision,'' he said. </P>
<P> A Serb liaison officer at Sarajevo airport objected to Jackovich taking a U.N. flight out of the city at the end of his assignment there and U.N. officials at the airport told the envoy there was nothing they could do about it. Instead they gave him an armored car to leave by road. </P>
<P> ``That's a very dangerous place for Americans to serve ... but I don't see any basis for adding to the risks and the dangers of Americans,'' Christopher said before talks with Mexican Foreign Minister Jose Angel Gurria. </P>
<P> Both men are attending a U.N. conference on nuclear non-proliferation. </P>
<P> Christopher said he would hold talks on Bosnia and other issues later Tuesday with British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd, French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe and German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel. One issue would be the future of the five-nation ``Contact Group'' which has sought unsuccessfully for the past year to bring peace to Bosnia. </P>
