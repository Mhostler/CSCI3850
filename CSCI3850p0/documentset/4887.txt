<TITLE> Greek ex-deputy police chief on trial for theft</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Greek ex-deputy police chief on trial for theft</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-GREECE-ANTIQUITIES </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>ATHENS (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Former Greek deputy police chief Michalis Nistikakis went on trial Monday on charges related to the attempted smuggling of ancient art treasures. </P>
<P> Nistikakis has been indicted along with eight other men on charges of acquiring objects from illegal excavations and attempting to sell 68 priceless artefacts dating from the fifth to the first century B.C. </P>
<P> The trial was expected to last two weeks and Nistikakis has been barred from leaving the country along with the other eight defendants, court officials said. </P>
<P> The smuggled antiquities included a marble lion head, silver coins portraying ancient emperors Alexander the Great and his father Philip, stone vessels and seals. </P>
<P> The case came to light in 1993 when police arrested a gang of smugglers who had planned to ship 281 antiquities to Munich. </P>
