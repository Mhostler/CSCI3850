<TITLE> Egypt's Nobel winner Mahfouz stabbed, wounded</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Egypt's Nobel winner Mahfouz stabbed, wounded</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-EGYPT-MAHFOUZ </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>CAIRO, Egypt (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Naguib Mahfouz, the only Arab winner of the Nobel literature prize, was stabbed and wounded Friday as he returned home, neighbors said. </P>
<P> The neighbors quoted witnesses as saying Mahfouz was approached by a group of men who looked as if they wanted to greet him but one stabbed him in the neck. He was taken to a nearby hospital. </P>
<P> Police at the hospital said Mahfouz, who won the Nobel prize in 1988, had a wound in the neck but it was not serious. </P>
<P> The confirmed reports by the residents that Mahfouz, 83, was attacked by a group of men who fled the scene in a car. The police said they had no idea about the motive of the attack. </P>
