<TITLE> Kim Il-sung's body said to be displayed in July</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Kim Il-sung's body said to be displayed in July</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-KOREA-BODY </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>SEOUL, April 26 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  North Korea is still embalming the body of late president Kim Il-sung and his mummified corpse will be put on display around the first anniversary of his death on July 8, Yonhap news agency said on Wednesday. </P>
<P> Yonhap, quoting an unnamed South Korean government official, said the embalming process was slow as North Korean officials were paying special attention to ensure Kim's body would be well preserved. </P>
<P> Russian and Chinese experts, who mummified Vladimir Lenin and Mao Tsetung, were still working on Kim's body at Pyongyang's presidential palace, the official was quoted as saying. </P>
<P> The domestic news agency did not say how the South Korean official found out about the plans for Kim's body. </P>
<P> Under the Russian method of embalming, all fluids are removed from the body and it is then dipped in a special preserving fluid. </P>
<P> The official was quoted as saying the body would be kept either in the presidential palace or in a special memorial centre on Pyongyang's Kim Il-sung Square. </P>
