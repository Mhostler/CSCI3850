<P> Col. Jan Dirk Van Mervelt, a spokesman for the U.N. Protection Force, said: ``As a precautionary move in defense of UNPROFOR personnel and the civilian population and as a routine precaution, aircraft were sent over the site. As far we know there were no air strikes.'' </P>
<P> United Nations officials said earlier the Bosnian town of Bihac, a U.N.-designated ``safe area,'' had come under Serb shelling attack at about 4 p.m. local time. </P>
<P> Serb forces have advanced on the Muslim-held town despite U.N. warnings and appeals to halt their assault on the so-called``safe area.'' </P>
<P> Bosnian Serb Army sources earlier said NATO aircraft were seen flying over the Bihac area. But they could not say if the jets had attacked any targets. </P>
