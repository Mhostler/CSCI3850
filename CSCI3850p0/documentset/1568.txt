<TITLE> Americans divided over Haiti policy, poll says</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Americans divided over Haiti policy, poll says</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-HAITI-USA-POLL </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>WASHINGTON (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  President Clinton's Haiti policy has divided Americans, a Los Angeles Times poll showed Friday. </P>
<P> The nationwide poll said 67 percent of the 1,340 people polled Wednesday and Thursday approved of the deal negotiated by former president Jimmy Carter's delegation that averted a last-minute invasion of Haiti. The poll reported 27 percent disapproved. </P>
<P> But of Clinton's decision to send up to 15,000 occupation troops, a majority, 53 percent, disapproved while 43 percent approved it with four percent undecided. </P>
<P> The poll's analysts said that Clinton's overall approval rating stands at 42 percent compared to 45 percent in a similar poll in July. His disapproval rating in the poll went from 47 percent in July to 52 percent in the latest survey. </P>
<P> Specifically on how people view Clinton's handling of the Haiti situation, 47 percent approved, while 44 percent disapproved and nine percent did not know. </P>
<P> The poll had a margin of error of plus or minus three percentage points. </P>
