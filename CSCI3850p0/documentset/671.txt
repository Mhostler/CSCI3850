<TITLE> EX-KGB SPY IN WASHINGTON DIES</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> EX-KGB SPY IN WASHINGTON DIES</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CIS-RUSSIA-AGENT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MOSCOW (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A Soviet-era spy who ran the KGB station in Washington and also headed the U.S. section of the security organization died in Moscow Tuesday, Itar-Tass news agency said. </P>
<P> It said Dmitry Yakushkin had died at the age of 71 after a long illness. </P>
<P> Yakushkin operated as Washington ``resident'' -- station chief -- for the KGB, the former Soviet Union's internal security and intelligence service. He also at one time headed the U.S. section of the first chief directorate of the KGB. </P>
<P> Tass did not say when Yakushkin held these posts, but it added he had also worked in Italy, Britain, Switzerland, Denmark, Sweden and India during 15 years' service abroad. </P>
