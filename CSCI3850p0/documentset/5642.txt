<TITLE> Veteran Chinese leader Chen Yun dead - paper</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Veteran Chinese leader Chen Yun dead - paper</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-CHINA-DEATH </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BEIJING (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Veteran revolutionary leader Chen Yun, one of China's leading economic planners and a colleague and later rival of paramount leader Deng Xiaoping, has died, Hong Kong's Ta Kung Pao said Tuesday. </P>
<P> He was 90. </P>
<P> Chen died in a Beijing hospital Monday, the newspaper said. It gave no other details of his death. </P>
<P> A Chinese source said Chen had long been suffering from blood problems, but was unable to give details. He had been ill for some time and had not appeared in public for several years. </P>
<P> Chen was second only to Deng in China's hierarchy of revolutionary veterans and had developed differences with him in recent years over the pace of his economic reforms. </P>
<P> His departure removes one of Deng's chief rivals and closest colleagues from China's political scene. </P>
<P> He is survived by his wife. His son Chen Yuan is deputy governor of the central bank and a daughter runs a venture capital company. </P>
