<TITLE> Angolans initial pact to end civil war</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Angolans initial pact to end civil war</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-ANGOLA-PEACE 1STLD </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES> (Eds: Adds Beye comment, background)</NOTES>
<DATELINE>LUSAKA, Zambia (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Angola's warring sides Monday initialed a pact mediated by the United Nations to end 19 years of fighting that has devastated the former Portuguese colony. </P>
<P> U.N. envoy Alioune Blondin Beye initialed the peace acccord in the Zambian capital along with representatives of the Luanda government and the UNITA rebel movement. </P>
<P> ``We are here to witness the signing of the protocol between the government and UNITA,'' Beye said at the ceremony. ``This evening we are formalising the document.'' </P>
<P> Delegations from the government and UNITA (National Union for the Total Liberation of Angola) began the U.N.-mediated talks in Lusaka in November last year. </P>
<P> The negotiations were marked by frequent stalemates and setbacks, the latest of which -- a dispute over security for UNITA leader Jonas Savimbi -- delayed the initialing ceremony several hours. </P>
<P> Savimbi and Angolan President Jose Eduardo dos Santos are expected formally to sign the peace accord in Lusaka in mid- November. </P>
<P> Monday night's ceremony in Lusaka was attended by Zambian Foreign Minister Remmy Musota and representatives of the United States, Russia and Portugal. </P>
