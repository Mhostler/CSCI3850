<TITLE> Canadian prime minister arrives in Brazil</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Canadian prime minister arrives in Brazil</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BRAZIL-CHRETIEN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BRASILIA, Brazil (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Canadian Prime Minister Jean Chretien arrived in Brazil Thursday on a visit aimed at strengthening trade ties with Latin America's biggest economy. </P>
<P> Chretien, accompanied by Foreign Trade Minister Roy McLaren, was greeted by Foreign Minister Luiz Felipe Lampreia. </P>
<P> He is leading a group of about 120 Canadian business executives on an 11-day visit to Latin America. </P>
<P> Chretien is scheduled to meet with President Fernando Henrique Cardoso Friday and go on to Rio de Janeiro Saturday. He leaves Sunday for Costa Rica. </P>
<P> The business delegation is primarily interested in opportunities in such areas as telecommunications, mining, energy and agribusiness, Canadian officials said. </P>
