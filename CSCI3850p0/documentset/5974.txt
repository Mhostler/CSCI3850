<TITLE> Non-Aligned to discuss Rwanda killings</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Non-Aligned to discuss Rwanda killings</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-RWANDA-NAM </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BANDUNG, Indonesia, April 24 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Foreign ministers of the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) will discuss this week the killings in Rawanda of thousands of people, Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas said on Monday. </P>
<P> Alatas, host of the meeting which begins on Tuesday, said the movement was very concerned about the weekend killings and would definitely discuss the issue during the gathering. </P>
<P> ``We are very concerned about what happened. It is certain we will discuss this issue,'' he told reporters. </P>
<P> Alatas was asked whether NAM, of which Rwanda is a member, would set up an investigating team. ``Let's see what we can do at this meeting,'' he replied. </P>
<P> The United Nations said some 2,000 Rwandanse were killed at a refugee camp either by gunfire from government soldiers or in a stampede it set off over the weekend. </P>
<P> Ministers held a commemorative ceremony on Monday to mark the 40th anniversary of the Asia-Africa Conference, which met in Bandung to seek a peaceful path through the Cold War and which led to the formation of the NAM six years later. </P>
<P> Foreign ministers will begin a three-day meeting here on Tuesday to discuss North-South issues including nuclear disarmament and restructuring of the United Nations. </P>
