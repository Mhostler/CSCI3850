<TITLE> IDB BANK, BRAZIL SIGN $20.5 MLN STREETCHILD PLAN</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> IDB BANK, BRAZIL SIGN $20.5 MLN STREETCHILD PLAN</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-BRAZIL-KIDS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>BRASILIA (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Interamerican Development Bank (IDB) and Brazilian officials were due to sign Tuesday night a $20.5 million agreement to help streetchildren and minors at risk of leaving home for the streets, Brazil's Foreign Affairs Ministry said. </P>
<P> The money, $18 million of which is provided by the IDB, will be spent on schools, creches, training centers, homes and other schemes in six cities across Brazil. </P>
