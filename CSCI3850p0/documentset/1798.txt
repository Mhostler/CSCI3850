<TITLE> Four convicted of attack on U.S. air base</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Four convicted of attack on U.S. air base</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-ITALY-BASE </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TRIESTE, Italy (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Four self-proclaimed members of the Red Brigades guerrilla group were sentenced to seven to 12 years in prison for an attack last year on a U.S. air base in northeastern Italy, court officials said Tuesday. </P>
<P> The four men were convicted of firing gunshots and throwing a grenade at a dormitory at Aviano, the main air base for U.S. fighter aircraft operating over Bosnia. No one was injured in the September 1993 attack. </P>
<P> During the trial in the northeastern city of Trieste, the defendents read proclamations stating they were members of the left-wing guerrilla group. </P>
<P> At the time of their arrest, police said the men belonged to a revived group of the Red Brigades, who in the 1970s and 1980s were responsible for a series of murders, shootings, and kidnappings. </P>
<P> The convictions and prison terms were decided by the court Monday night. </P>
