<TITLE> Aftershock shakes northern Japan, no damage</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Aftershock shakes northern Japan, no damage</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-QUAKE-JAPAN </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TOKYO, April 2 (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  A fairly strong earthquake measuring 5.2 on the Richter scale shook parts of northern Japan on Sunday morning, but no injuries or damage were reported immediately. </P>
<P> The tremor, which occurred at 0129 GMT, was thought to be an aftershock of an earthquake that jolted the area around Niigata on Saturday. </P>
<P> There was no danger of tidal waves, the Meteorological Agency said. </P>
<P> Saturday's earthquake measured 6.0 on the open-ended Richter scale. It injured 36 people and damaged 259 houses, almost destroying five. </P>
<P> An earthquake measuring 7.2 on the Richter-scale hit western Japan in January, claiming 5,500 lives. Many of the victims of the quake were residents of the port city of Kobe. </P>
