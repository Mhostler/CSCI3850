<TITLE> ETA CLAIMS RESPONSIBILITY FOR MADRID CAR BOMB</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> ETA CLAIMS RESPONSIBILITY FOR MADRID CAR BOMB</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-SPAIN-ETA </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>MADRID, Spain (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  The Basque separatist guerrilla group ETA said Tuesday it was responsible for a car bomb which killed a top-ranking army general, his driver and a civilian in the heart of Madrid last Friday. </P>
<P> In a statement published in the radical Basque paper Egin, ETA also said it killed Jose Manuel Olarte, shot as he sat playing cards in a restaurant in San Sebastian last Wednesday. </P>
<P> ETA accused Olarte of being a drug dealer and an informer. </P>
<P> General Francisco Veguillas, director-general of defense policy, was killed when a car bomb exploded as his armor-plated vehicle was passing through a small square in Madrid. Fourteen people were injured in the rush-hour blast. </P>
<P> ETA (Basque Homeland and Freedom) have killed around 800 people in their 25-year armed campaign for an independent Basque homeland. </P>
