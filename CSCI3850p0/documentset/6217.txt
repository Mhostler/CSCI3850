<P> The agreement followed U.N.-brokered talks between the local Serb officer and Croatian vice-president, Ivica Kostovic, in the divided town of Pakrac, Manuel said. ``He has agreed to hand over the arms of the men under his command.'' </P>
<P> The Krajina Serb officer, identified as Lt.-Col. Harambasic, made the verbal agreement on behalf of Serb troops deployed along the former U.N. cease-fire line on the northern edge of the western Slavonia region. </P>
<P> The Serb soldiers would begin handing over artillery, mortars and other big guns Wednesday to U.N. Argentine troops. </P>
<P> Although fighting had subsided throughout the enclave it was unclear if Serb forces remaining in the southern part of the pocket would agree to surrender to Zagreb authorities, the U.N. spokeswoman said. </P>
