<SUBJECT> BC-YUGOSLAVIA-AIRPORT-ARRIVAL -2 SARAJEVO </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> The second of two scheduled U.N. flights was due to arrive later in the day but the first relief plane was not expected before next week. </P>
<P> The Bosnian Serb Army had earlier frustrated the U.N. by refusing clearance for Saturday's flights. </P>
<P> Radovan Karadzic, president of the self-proclaimed Bosnian Serb republic, promised the airport would be allowed to reopen as part of a package of measures to spur peace talks. </P>
<P> Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter said Saturday he was traveling to Bosnia over the weekend to mediate between Karadzic and the Muslim-led Bosnian government. </P>
