<TITLE> New Jersey indicts oil businessmen for tax evasion</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> New Jersey indicts oil businessmen for tax evasion</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-INDICTMENT </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>TRENTON, N.J. (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  An oil business family and six associates were indicted for evading federal and state taxes, Attorney General Deborah Poritz announced Thursday. </P>
<P> Charged as the mastermind was Christopher Grungo, 44, of Philadelphia, owner of the now defunct Noble Oil Co. in Tabernacle, N.J. </P>
<P> Grungo's alleged scheme involved the commingling of waste oil with fuel oil and is alleged to have cost the federal treasury $798,000 and the state $158,800. </P>
<P> Also indicted were Frank V. Grungo Sr., 76, of Tabernacle, N.J. and Brenda L. Grungo, 34, of Southampton, N.J. </P>
<P> Ronald Musser Sr., 58, and Gerald Olkus, 48, of Philadelphia, Paul C. McDaniel, 46, of West Chester, Pa., and three New Jerseyans, Russell Cerminara, 51, of Cherry Hill, Frederick Fisher, 58, of Hammonton and James Barbone, 41, of West Berlin, were accused of profiting from the plot. </P>
<P> Musser, Olkus and McDaniel were charged with providing home heating oil for resale. Fisher was charged with transporting the oil, Cerminara with selling it and Frank and Brenda Grungo with facilitating the movement of checks and false paperwork. </P>
<P> If convicted, the nine defendants face 20 years in jail and $200,000 fines. </P>
