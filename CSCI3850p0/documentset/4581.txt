<SUBJECT> BC-BANK-MARKETS-JAPAN -2 TOKYO </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE></DATELINE>
<P> Barings, London bankers to customers including Queen Elizabeth II, went bust Sunday under losses of more than $750 million that were run up in speculative trade by a maverick Singapore-based trader. </P>
<P> Banking sources said the trader apparently ran up losses on trading in derivatives based on Tokyo share prices. </P>
<P> Derivatives -- futures, options and swaps -- are contracts linked to values of commodities, currencies, shares or bonds. </P>
<P> They were designed as hedges against price fluctuation but profit on speculative trade in them can be huge. So can losses. </P>
