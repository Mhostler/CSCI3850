<TITLE> Bomb blows up home of President Fujimori's aide</TITLE>
<HEADLINE> Bomb blows up home of President Fujimori's aide</HEADLINE>
<SUBJECT> BC-PERU-BOMBS </SUBJECT>
<AUTHOR></AUTHOR>
<TEXT>
<NOTES></NOTES>
<DATELINE>LIMA, Peru (Reuter) </DATELINE>
<P>  Suspected Maoist rebels blew up the house of an aide to Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori and detonated another bomb outside a factory, causing material damage but only minor injuries, police said Saturday. </P>
<P> A 50-pound dynamite explosive device late Friday caused severe damage to the house of Segifredo Lusa, a member of Peru's intelligence service and an advisor to Fujimori on intelligence matters. </P>
<P> The house was severely damaged but no-one was injured. It was not immediately clear whether there was anyone home at the time of the explosion. </P>
<P> Earlier in the day a car bomb containing 160 pounds of dynamite was detonated outside a Lima chocolate factory, causing damage but only minor injuries to neighbors. </P>
<P> Police said it suspected Shining Path guerrillas. </P>
<P> Also on Friday, an explosive device was found on Lima's international airport runway, forcing the rerouting of a plane carrying a group of visiting Bolivian military officials. </P>
<P> Police defused the device but refused to speculate on who had planted it or whether there was any connection with the Bolivian military. </P>
